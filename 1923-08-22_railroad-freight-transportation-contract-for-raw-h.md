# FREIGHT TRANSPORTATION CONTRACT

**BETWEEN**

**BOSTON & MAINE RAILROAD CORPORATION**  
North Station  
Boston, Massachusetts  

**AND**

**JOHN J. RILEY COMPANY**  
228 Salem Street  
East Woburn, Massachusetts  

---

## ARTICLE I - PARTIES AND RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Boston & Maine Railroad Corporation ("Railroad"), a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws of the State of Massachusetts, operates railroad freight transportation services throughout New England; and

**WHEREAS**, John J. Riley Company ("Shipper"), a Massachusetts business enterprise engaged in the tanning and processing of animal hides at its facility located at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts, requires regular and reliable transportation services for the shipment of raw hides and related materials to its manufacturing facility; and

**WHEREAS**, the parties desire to establish terms and conditions for the transportation of raw hides, salt, and other tanning materials via railroad freight service from various origins to the Shipper's facility in East Woburn;

**NOW THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II - SCOPE OF SERVICES

The Railroad agrees to provide freight transportation services for the following commodities:

1. **Raw cattle hides** from stockyards and slaughterhouses in Boston, Worcester, Springfield, and other New England locations
2. **Raw sheep and lamb hides** from regional suppliers
3. **Salt for hide preservation** in bulk quantities
4. **Tanning chemicals and solutions** including but not limited to:
   - Sodium chloride
   - Lime preparations
   - Bark extracts
   - Chrome tanning agents
   - Various organic solvents as may be required for leather processing

The Railroad shall transport said commodities to the freight siding adjacent to the Shipper's facility at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts, utilizing the existing spur line that connects to the main Boston & Maine line running through the Aberjona Valley.

## ARTICLE III - RATES AND CHARGES

**Section 3.1 - Base Freight Rates**

The following rates shall apply for transportation services, calculated per hundredweight (cwt):

- Raw hides: $0.85 per cwt for distances up to 50 miles; $1.15 per cwt for distances exceeding 50 miles
- Salt (bulk): $0.60 per cwt regardless of distance within New England service area
- Chemical solutions: $1.25 per cwt, with additional handling charges of $0.15 per cwt for hazardous materials requiring special care

**Section 3.2 - Additional Charges**

The Shipper shall be responsible for the following additional charges:

- Demurrage charges of $2.50 per car per day for detention of freight cars beyond the free time allowance of 48 hours
- Switching charges of $3.75 per car for placement and removal of cars at the East Woburn siding
- Special handling charges for commodities requiring refrigeration or expedited service

**Section 3.3 - Payment Terms**

All freight charges shall be payable within thirty (30) days of invoice date. The Railroad reserves the right to require prepayment or satisfactory credit arrangements for shipments exceeding $500.00 in total freight charges.

## ARTICLE IV - DELIVERY AND PERFORMANCE

**Section 4.1 - Delivery Schedule**

The Railroad shall provide regular freight service to the East Woburn facility with deliveries scheduled as follows:

- **Monday, Wednesday, and Friday**: Raw hide shipments from Boston area suppliers
- **Tuesday and Thursday**: Chemical and salt deliveries
- **Saturday**: Special shipments and overflow as required

The Railroad shall use reasonable efforts to maintain this schedule but shall not be liable for delays caused by weather, mechanical failure, labor disputes, or other circumstances beyond its reasonable control.

**Section 4.2 - Delivery Location**

All shipments shall be delivered to the freight siding located on the western boundary of the Shipper's property at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn. The siding extends approximately 400 feet along the main line and can accommodate up to three (3) standard freight cars simultaneously.

## ARTICLE V - RESPONSIBILITIES AND LIABILITIES

**Section 5.1 - Shipper's Responsibilities**

The Shipper shall:

1. Provide accurate and complete shipping instructions for all commodities
2. Ensure proper packaging and preparation of all materials for shipment
3. Maintain the freight siding and unloading facilities in safe and serviceable condition
4. Unload freight cars within the prescribed free time allowance
5. Comply with all applicable regulations regarding the handling of chemicals and hazardous materials

**Section 5.2 - Railroad's Responsibilities**

The Railroad shall:

1. Transport all commodities with reasonable care and dispatch
2. Provide freight cars suitable for the commodities being shipped
3. Maintain accurate records of all shipments and charges
4. Notify the Shipper of any delays or service disruptions that may affect delivery schedules

**Section 5.3 - Limitation of Liability**

The Railroad's liability for loss or damage to commodities in transit shall be limited to the actual value of the commodities as declared by the Shipper, not to exceed $100.00 per hundredweight unless a higher value is declared and additional charges paid therefor.

## ARTICLE VI - TERM AND TERMINATION

This contract shall commence on September 1, 1923, and shall continue for a period of three (3) years, terminating on August 31, 1926, unless sooner terminated in accordance with the provisions hereof. Either party may terminate this contract upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party.

## ARTICLE VII - SPECIAL PROVISIONS

**Section 7.1 - Seasonal Adjustments**

Recognizing the seasonal nature of the hide processing business, the parties acknowledge that shipment volumes may vary significantly throughout the year. During peak seasons (typically October through March), the Railroad agrees to provide additional car capacity as available to accommodate increased shipping requirements.

**Section 7.2 - Equipment Specifications**

For shipments of chemical solutions and solvents, the Railroad shall provide tank cars or specially lined box cars as appropriate for the specific commodities being transported. The Shipper shall provide reasonable advance notice of any special equipment requirements.

## ARTICLE VIII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**Section 8.1 - Governing Law**

This contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and applicable federal railroad regulations.

**Section 8.2 - Entire Agreement**

This contract constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

**Section 8.3 - Amendment**

This contract may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.
