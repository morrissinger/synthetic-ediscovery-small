# TECHNICAL ANALYSIS REPORT

**ORGANIC SOLVENT MANUFACTURING PROCESS REVIEW AND RECOMMENDATIONS**

**SUBJECT:** Quarterly Process Review - Organic Solvent Operations

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents findings from the quarterly review of organic solvent manufacturing processes at our Woburn facility, conducted between October 15-November 15, 1968. The analysis encompasses production efficiency metrics, waste stream characteristics, and operational recommendations for the upcoming fiscal quarter. Key findings indicate opportunities for process optimization while maintaining current production volumes of trichloroethylene (TCE), perchloroethylene (PCE), and related chlorinated compounds essential to our packaging film manufacturing operations.

## INTRODUCTION

The Cryovac Division's Woburn manufacturing facility has operated continuously since 1964, producing specialized organic solvents critical to our proprietary plastic film manufacturing processes. Our current production capacity of 2,400 gallons per day across multiple solvent lines represents a significant component of W.R. Grace Company's industrial chemical portfolio in the northeastern region. This quarterly review examines operational parameters, identifies process improvements, and addresses waste management protocols in accordance with standard industrial practices.

## METHODOLOGY

### Production Data Analysis
Process data was collected from October 15 through November 15, 1968, encompassing 744 operational hours across three production shifts. Key performance indicators monitored included:

- Raw material consumption rates
- Product yield percentages
- Energy consumption per unit output
- Waste generation volumes
- Equipment downtime incidents

### Sampling Procedures
Representative samples were collected every 8 hours from primary production lines, with additional samples taken during equipment maintenance periods. Chemical analysis was performed using standard laboratory protocols established by the American Chemical Society for industrial solvent purity verification.

### Waste Stream Characterization
Comprehensive analysis of liquid waste streams was conducted to quantify organic compound concentrations and determine optimal disposal methodologies. Sampling points included primary reactor overflow, equipment cleaning stations, and floor drain collection systems.

## FINDINGS

### Production Efficiency Metrics

Current production levels demonstrate satisfactory performance across all solvent manufacturing lines:

**Trichloroethylene Production Line:**
- Average daily output: 950 gallons
- Product purity: 99.2% (within specification)
- Raw material utilization: 94.7%
- Unplanned downtime: 2.3 hours per week

**Perchloroethylene Production Line:**
- Average daily output: 780 gallons
- Product purity: 98.9% (within specification)
- Raw material utilization: 92.1%
- Unplanned downtime: 4.1 hours per week

**Mixed Chlorinated Compounds:**
- Average daily output: 670 gallons
- Product purity: 97.8% (acceptable range)
- Raw material utilization: 89.3%
- Unplanned downtime: 6.7 hours per week

### Waste Stream Analysis

Liquid waste generation during the review period totaled approximately 15,200 gallons, representing 6.3% of total production volume. Chemical composition analysis reveals the following constituent breakdown:

- Trichloroethylene: 2,840 ppm average concentration
- Perchloroethylene: 1,960 ppm average concentration
- 1,1,1-Trichloroethane: 890 ppm average concentration
- Trans-1,2-Dichloroethylene: 340 ppm average concentration
- Various chlorinated organic compounds: 1,120 ppm combined

Current disposal methodology involves temporary storage in concrete-lined containment areas adjacent to the main production building, with periodic removal via contracted waste hauling services. This approach aligns with standard industrial practices for organic solvent waste management and provides cost-effective disposal solutions.

### Equipment Performance Assessment

Primary manufacturing equipment demonstrated reliable performance throughout the review period, with scheduled maintenance activities completed on time. Notable observations include:

1. **Reactor Vessel Efficiency:** Primary reaction chambers maintained optimal temperature and pressure parameters 96.8% of operational time.

2. **Distillation Column Performance:** Separation efficiency remained within acceptable ranges, though minor fluctuations were observed during periods of high ambient humidity.

3. **Cooling System Operations:** Heat exchange systems functioned adequately, with cooling water consumption averaging 18,400 gallons per day.

### Quality Control Metrics

Product quality maintained consistent standards throughout the evaluation period. Random sampling indicated compliance with customer specifications across all product lines. Minor variations in chlorinated compound concentrations fell within acceptable tolerance ranges and did not impact downstream manufacturing processes.

## OPERATIONAL CHALLENGES

Several operational challenges were identified during the review period:

1. **Raw Material Supply Consistency:** Periodic delays in chlorine gas deliveries resulted in temporary production adjustments. Coordination with suppliers should be enhanced to minimize future disruptions.

2. **Waste Storage Capacity:** Current containment facilities approach maximum capacity during peak production periods. Additional storage infrastructure may be required to accommodate projected production increases.

3. **Equipment Maintenance Scheduling:** Competing demands for specialized maintenance personnel occasionally result in delayed routine maintenance activities.

## RECOMMENDATIONS

### Process Optimization Opportunities

Based on the analysis conducted, the following recommendations are proposed for implementation during the next operational quarter:

**1. Enhanced Raw Material Inventory Management**
Implement a 15-day minimum inventory buffer for critical raw materials, particularly chlorine gas and organic precursor compounds. This approach will minimize production disruptions and maintain consistent output levels.

**2. Waste Stream Consolidation**
Investigate opportunities to combine compatible waste streams prior to disposal, potentially reducing overall waste management costs by 12-18%. Initial analysis suggests that certain chlorinated compounds can be co-disposed without regulatory complications.

**3. Equipment Upgrade Evaluation**
Consider replacement of aging distillation column components during the next scheduled maintenance shutdown. Upgraded internals could improve separation efficiency and reduce energy consumption by an estimated 8-10%.

### Waste Management Protocol Updates

Current waste disposal practices should be maintained, as they provide effective management of organic solvent byproducts while remaining cost-competitive with alternative disposal methods. The existing concrete containment system adequately prevents environmental release while awaiting periodic removal by certified waste hauling contractors.

Consideration should be given to expanding temporary storage capacity through installation of additional concrete-lined containment areas. This expansion would provide operational flexibility during peak production periods and reduce the frequency of waste removal scheduling requirements.

### Production Capacity Planning

Projected demand for organic solvents suggests potential for 15-20% production increases over the next 18 months. Current equipment capacity can accommodate this growth with minimal capital investment, primarily focused on enhanced raw material handling systems and expanded temporary waste storage facilities.

## CONCLUSIONS

The Woburn facility's organic solvent manufacturing operations continue to meet production targets while maintaining acceptable quality standards. The identified optimization opportunities present viable pathways for enhanced operational efficiency without compromising product quality or increasing operational complexity.

Waste management protocols remain appropriate for current production volumes and regulatory requirements. The temporary storage and periodic removal approach provides cost-effective disposal solutions while maintaining operational flexibility.

Equipment performance demonstrates the reliability of current manufacturing systems, with routine maintenance schedules adequate to support continued operations. Minor adjustments to maintenance timing and raw material inventory management will address the operational challenges identified during this review period.

The recommendations presented provide a framework for sustained production growth while maintaining our commitment to efficient manufacturing practices.
