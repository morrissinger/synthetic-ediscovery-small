## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Supplier is engaged in the business of manufacturing, distributing, and selling industrial chemicals and solvents; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser operates a manufacturing facility in Woburn, Massachusetts, requiring tetrachloroethylene for industrial cleaning and degreasing operations; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser desires to establish a reliable supply arrangement for said chemical substance; and

WHEREAS, Supplier has the capacity and expertise to provide tetrachloroethylene meeting Purchaser's specifications and delivery requirements;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I - PRODUCT SPECIFICATIONS

**1.1 Chemical Product:** Supplier shall provide tetrachloroethylene (perchloroethylene, PCE) meeting the following specifications:
- Purity: Minimum 99.5% by volume
- Water content: Maximum 0.05%
- Acidity (as HCl): Maximum 0.003%
- Color: Water white to pale yellow
- Specific gravity: 1.622 ± 0.005 at 20°C

**1.2 Packaging:** Product shall be delivered in steel drums of 55-gallon capacity, meeting Department of Transportation specifications for hazardous materials transport. Each drum shall be clearly labeled with product identification, hazard warnings, and lot numbers.

**1.3 Quality Assurance:** Each shipment shall include a Certificate of Analysis confirming compliance with specifications set forth in Section 1.1.

## ARTICLE II - DELIVERY AND QUANTITY

**2.1 Quantity:** Purchaser agrees to purchase a minimum of 2,200 gallons (40 drums) of tetrachloroethylene per month, with maximum monthly quantity not to exceed 5,500 gallons (100 drums).

**2.2 Delivery Schedule:** Deliveries shall be made bi-weekly on Tuesdays and Fridays, with advance notice of 48 hours. Standard delivery quantity shall be 20 drums per delivery unless otherwise specified by Purchaser.

**2.3 Delivery Location:** All deliveries shall be made to Purchaser's facility at 15 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts, to the designated chemical receiving area adjacent to Building C.

**2.4 Transportation:** Supplier shall arrange and pay for all transportation costs via licensed hazardous materials carriers. All shipments must comply with Interstate Commerce Commission regulations and Massachusetts Department of Public Safety requirements.

## ARTICLE III - PRICING AND PAYMENT

**3.1 Price:** The price for tetrachloroethylene shall be $0.47 per gallon, F.O.B. destination, based on current market conditions as of the date of this Agreement.

**3.2 Price Adjustments:** Prices may be adjusted quarterly based on changes in raw material costs, with thirty (30) days written notice to Purchaser. No price increase shall exceed 8% in any twelve-month period.

**3.3 Payment Terms:** Payment shall be net thirty (30) days from invoice date. A 2% discount shall apply for payments received within ten (10) days of invoice date.

**3.4 Late Payment:** Overdue amounts shall bear interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

## ARTICLE IV - HANDLING AND SAFETY

**4.1 Material Safety:** Supplier warrants that tetrachloroethylene delivered hereunder shall be suitable for Purchaser's intended industrial cleaning applications when used in accordance with generally accepted industry practices.

**4.2 Safety Documentation:** Supplier shall provide current Material Safety Data Sheets and handling instructions with each delivery, including information on proper storage, ventilation requirements, and disposal considerations.

**4.3 Regulatory Compliance:** Both parties acknowledge their respective obligations to comply with applicable federal, state, and local regulations governing the manufacture, transport, storage, use, and disposal of tetrachloroethylene, including but not limited to regulations promulgated by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration and the Department of Transportation.

**4.4 Purchaser Responsibilities:** Purchaser agrees to maintain adequate storage facilities, implement appropriate safety procedures, and ensure proper training of personnel handling the chemical product.

## ARTICLE V - WASTE DISPOSAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS

**5.1 Waste Generation:** Purchaser acknowledges that use of tetrachloroethylene may generate waste materials requiring proper disposal in accordance with applicable environmental regulations.

**5.2 Disposal Methods:** Purchaser represents that any waste materials containing tetrachloroethylene will be disposed of through licensed waste disposal contractors or other methods approved by appropriate regulatory authorities.

**5.3 Environmental Compliance:** Purchaser agrees to comply with all applicable environmental laws and regulations in connection with the storage, use, and disposal of tetrachloroethylene and related waste materials.

## ARTICLE VI - TERM AND TERMINATION

**6.1 Term:** This Agreement shall commence on October 1, 1969, and shall continue for a period of two (2) years, unless terminated earlier in accordance with the provisions hereof.

**6.2 Renewal:** This Agreement may be renewed for additional one-year periods by mutual written consent of the parties, executed not less than sixty (60) days prior to expiration.

**6.3 Termination:** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party.

## ARTICLE VII - WARRANTIES AND LIABILITY

**7.1 Product Warranty:** Supplier warrants that the tetrachloroethylene delivered hereunder shall conform to the specifications set forth in Article I and shall be free from defects in material and workmanship.

**7.2 Limitation of Liability:** Supplier's liability for any claim arising from or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the purchase price of the specific product giving rise to such claim.

**7.3 Indemnification:** Each party agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the other party from and against any claims, damages, or expenses arising from such party's negligent acts or omissions in the performance of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE VIII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**8.1 Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

**8.2 Force Majeure:** Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance resulting from acts of God, war, terrorism, labor disputes, or other causes beyond the reasonable control of such party.

**8.3 Assignment:** This Agreement may not be assigned by either party without the prior written consent of the other party.

**8.4 Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

**8.5 Amendment:** This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.
