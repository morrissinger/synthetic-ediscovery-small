# CITY OF WOBURN WATER COMMISSION
## REGULATORY APPROVAL NOTICE

**TO:** Wells G and H Operations Department  
**FROM:** City Water Commission  
**DATE:** August 19, 1977  
**RE:** Continued Operating Permit for Municipal Wells G and H  
**PERMIT NO:** WC-77-0819-GH  
**FILING REF:** 301.4-1977-0819

---

## REGULATORY AUTHORITY

The City of Woburn Water Commission, acting under the authority granted by Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 111, Section 160 et seq., and pursuant to the Municipal Water Supply Regulations (310 CMR 22.00), hereby issues this regulatory approval for the continued operation of Municipal Wells G and H located in the East Woburn district.

## BACKGROUND AND FINDINGS

WHEREAS, Municipal Wells G and H commenced operations in 1964 and 1966 respectively, providing supplemental water supply to the City of Woburn municipal distribution system; and

WHEREAS, said wells have maintained continuous operation for a period exceeding ten (10) years, providing essential water resources to residential and commercial customers in the East Woburn service area; and

WHEREAS, the Water Commission has conducted its annual review of operational parameters, water quality testing protocols, and distribution system performance in accordance with established municipal procedures; and

WHEREAS, the wells continue to demonstrate adequate flow rates and pressure maintenance capabilities necessary for municipal service obligations; and

WHEREAS, routine bacteriological testing conducted by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health laboratory has shown compliance with established standards for coliform bacteria detection; and

WHEREAS, the Commission has reviewed maintenance records, equipment performance logs, and operational reports submitted by Wells G and H Operations Department for the period January 1976 through July 1977;

NOW THEREFORE, the City Water Commission hereby grants continued operating approval for Wells G and H under the following terms and conditions:

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF APPROVAL

### Section 1: Operational Requirements

1.1 **Continuous Operation Authorization**: Wells G and H are hereby authorized to continue pumping operations at current capacity levels, with maximum combined daily production not to exceed 5.7 million gallons per day.

1.2 **Water Quality Monitoring**: Operations Department shall continue monthly bacteriological sampling as required by Massachusetts Department of Public Health regulations, with samples collected at wellhead locations and submitted to certified laboratory facilities.

1.3 **Equipment Maintenance**: All pumping equipment, electrical systems, and distribution infrastructure shall be maintained according to manufacturer specifications and industry standard practices.

1.4 **Operational Records**: Detailed logs of daily production volumes, equipment maintenance, and any operational irregularities shall be maintained and made available for Commission review upon request.

### Section 2: Regulatory Compliance

2.1 **State Health Department Coordination**: Operations Department shall maintain regular communication with Massachusetts Department of Public Health regarding water quality testing requirements and shall promptly report any test results indicating potential contamination.

2.2 **Municipal Code Compliance**: All operations shall comply with City of Woburn municipal ordinances, including but not limited to noise regulations, traffic control measures, and property maintenance standards.

2.3 **Emergency Response Protocols**: Operations Department shall maintain current emergency response procedures for equipment failures, power outages, and other operational disruptions that could affect water supply continuity.

### Section 3: Quality Assurance

3.1 **Testing Protocols**: Standard bacteriological testing shall continue on monthly intervals, with additional testing as deemed necessary by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health or City Water Commission.

3.2 **Distribution System Monitoring**: Regular monitoring of water pressure, flow rates, and system integrity shall be conducted throughout the East Woburn distribution network.

3.3 **Customer Service Standards**: Operations Department shall maintain response capabilities for customer complaints regarding water quality, pressure, or service interruptions.

### Section 4: Reporting Requirements

4.1 **Monthly Reports**: Operations Department shall submit monthly operational reports to the Water Commission, including production volumes, maintenance activities, and any operational issues encountered.

4.2 **Annual Review**: A comprehensive annual review of well performance, water quality data, and system reliability shall be conducted by the Water Commission each August.

4.3 **Incident Reporting**: Any equipment failures, contamination concerns, or service disruptions shall be reported to the Water Commission within twenty-four (24) hours of occurrence.

## DURATION AND RENEWAL

This regulatory approval shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from the date of issuance, expiring on August 19, 1979, unless renewed by the Water Commission following standard review procedures. Operations Department may submit renewal application beginning sixty (60) days prior to expiration date.

## REGULATORY FINDINGS

The Water Commission finds that Wells G and H continue to serve an essential function in the municipal water supply system, providing reliable water resources to the East Woburn community. The wells have demonstrated consistent operational performance and have maintained compliance with applicable health and safety regulations throughout their operational history.

The Commission notes that both wells have provided uninterrupted service to residential customers in the Montvale Road area, Pine Street corridor, and surrounding neighborhoods, supporting the municipal obligation to provide adequate water supply to all citizens within the service territory.

Based upon review of operational records, maintenance logs, and water quality testing data, the Commission determines that continued operation of Wells G and H serves the public interest and supports the municipal duty to provide safe and adequate water supply to residents and businesses in the affected service area.

## COMMISSION AUTHORITY

This approval is issued under the regulatory authority of the City Water Commission as established by municipal charter and Massachusetts state law. The Commission retains the authority to modify, suspend, or revoke this approval should circumstances warrant such action to protect public health and safety.

## EFFECTIVE DATE

This regulatory approval shall take effect immediately upon issuance and shall govern all operations of Wells G and H until expiration or renewal as provided herein.
