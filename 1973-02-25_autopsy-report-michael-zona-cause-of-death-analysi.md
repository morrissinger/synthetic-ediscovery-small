## CIRCUMSTANCES OF DEATH

The decedent was a 4-year-old male who had been under treatment at Massachusetts General Hospital for acute lymphoblastic leukemia since initial diagnosis in September 1972. According to hospital records provided by Dr. Robert Chen, attending pediatric oncologist, the child had been admitted for his final hospitalization on February 18, 1973, presenting with severe anemia, thrombocytopenia, and signs of systemic infection secondary to immunocompromise related to his underlying hematologic malignancy.

The child's medical history indicates normal development until age 3 years, at which time the family noted increasing fatigue, pallor, and frequent minor infections. Initial laboratory studies in August 1972 revealed a white blood cell count of 47,000/μL with 85% lymphoblasts. Bone marrow biopsy confirmed the diagnosis of acute lymphoblastic leukemia. Treatment included multiple courses of chemotherapy per standard pediatric protocols of the time.

Death occurred on February 23, 1973, at 14:45 hours following cardiopulmonary arrest secondary to septic shock and multi-organ failure in the setting of severe immunosuppression.

## EXTERNAL EXAMINATION

The body is that of a well-developed but markedly underweight Caucasian male child appearing consistent with the stated age of 4 years, 7 months. Length is 97 cm (38.2 inches). Weight is 12.8 kg (28.2 pounds), representing significant weight loss from normal parameters for age. The body is well-preserved and shows no evidence of decomposition.

**General Appearance:** The child appears chronically ill with marked pallor of the skin and mucous membranes. Multiple small petechial hemorrhages are noted on the chest, arms, and neck, consistent with thrombocytopenia. Several healing venipuncture sites are present on both arms and the right external jugular region.

**Head and Neck:** The scalp shows diffuse alopecia consistent with chemotherapy treatment. No traumatic injuries are identified. The conjunctivae are markedly pale. Oral examination reveals multiple small ulcerative lesions on the tongue and buccal mucosa, consistent with chemotherapy-induced mucositis.

**Chest:** Multiple healing surgical scars are present, including a right subclavian central venous catheter insertion site. No acute traumatic injuries identified.

**Abdomen:** Soft and non-distended. A well-healed surgical scar is present in the right lower quadrant, consistent with previous bone marrow biopsy procedures.

**Extremities:** Marked muscle wasting is present in all extremities. Multiple healing venipuncture sites noted. No acute injuries identified.

## INTERNAL EXAMINATION

**Cardiovascular System:** The heart weighs 85 grams (normal range 60-90g for age). The myocardium appears pale but shows no acute pathologic changes. Coronary arteries are patent. No congenital abnormalities identified. Minimal pericardial fluid present.

**Respiratory System:** Both lungs are markedly congested and edematous, with patchy areas of consolidation consistent with bronchopneumonia. The right lung weighs 180 grams, the left lung weighs 165 grams (combined weight elevated for age). Multiple small hemorrhagic foci are present throughout both lung fields.

**Hematopoietic System:** The spleen is enlarged, weighing 95 grams (normal 40-60g for age). The cut surface shows a homogeneous, soft, pale appearance with loss of normal architectural landmarks. Multiple small hemorrhagic foci are present.

The bone marrow, examined from samples taken from the sternum and iliac crest, shows near-complete replacement of normal hematopoietic elements with immature lymphoid cells consistent with acute lymphoblastic leukemia. Normal erythroid and myeloid precursors are markedly reduced.

**Hepatobiliary System:** The liver weighs 520 grams (normal 400-500g for age). The capsular surface is smooth. Cut sections reveal a pale, slightly enlarged organ with no focal lesions. Microscopic examination shows mild periportal lymphoid infiltration consistent with leukemic involvement.

**Genitourinary System:** Both kidneys appear normal in size and configuration. Combined weight is 125 grams (normal range 110-130g for age). Cut surfaces show normal corticomedullary differentiation with no gross abnormalities.

**Gastrointestinal System:** The stomach and small intestine show multiple small ulcerative lesions consistent with chemotherapy-induced mucositis. No evidence of perforation or significant hemorrhage.

**Central Nervous System:** The brain weighs 1,180 grams (normal range 1,100-1,200g for age). External examination shows mild cerebral edema but no focal lesions. Cut sections reveal no gross abnormalities. No evidence of central nervous system leukemic involvement identified on gross examination.

## MICROSCOPIC EXAMINATION

**Bone Marrow:** Sections show near-complete effacement of normal hematopoietic architecture with replacement by sheets of immature lymphoid cells. These cells are characterized by high nuclear-to-cytoplasmic ratios, irregular nuclear contours, and prominent nucleoli. Mitotic figures are frequent. Normal megakaryocytes, erythroid precursors, and mature myeloid elements are markedly reduced.

**Spleen:** Microscopic examination reveals diffuse infiltration of the white pulp and red pulp by immature lymphoid cells morphologically identical to those seen in the bone marrow. Normal splenic architecture is largely obliterated.

**Liver:** Sections show mild periportal and sinusoidal infiltration by leukemic cells. No significant hepatocellular necrosis is identified.

**Lungs:** Sections reveal acute bronchopneumonia with neutrophilic infiltration of alveolar spaces. Multiple small hemorrhagic foci are present. Some alveolar spaces contain proteinaceous edema fluid.

**Lymph Nodes:** Available cervical lymph node tissue shows complete architectural effacement with replacement by leukemic lymphoblasts.
