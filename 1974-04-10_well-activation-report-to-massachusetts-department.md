# WELL ACTIVATION REPORT

**TO:** Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality  
**FROM:** City of Woburn Department of Public Works  
**DATE:** April 10, 1974  
**RE:** Activation of Municipal Wells G and H - Quarterly Status Report

**Filing Reference:** DEQ-WOB-74-0410-WAR  
**Municipal License:** MW-2847-WOBURN  
**Regulatory Citation:** 310 CMR 22.00 - Public Water Supply Standards

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The City of Woburn Department of Public Works hereby submits this quarterly well activation report for municipal Wells G and H, located in the East Woburn district, pursuant to Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality regulations governing public water supply operations. This report covers the period January 1, 1974 through March 31, 1974, and documents continued operational status of both wells within approved parameters.

Wells G and H remain critical components of the municipal water distribution system, providing approximately 2.4 million gallons per day to serve the growing residential population in East Woburn. Both wells continue to meet established flow requirements and maintain water pressure sufficient for residential and light commercial use throughout their service area.

## WELL OPERATIONAL STATUS

### Well G (Activated May 22, 1964)
- **Location:** East Woburn, adjacent to industrial corridor
- **Depth:** 180 feet
- **Current Flow Rate:** 1,200 gallons per minute
- **Daily Pumping Volume:** 1.3 million gallons average
- **Operational Hours:** 18 hours daily average
- **Maintenance Status:** Routine quarterly inspection completed March 15, 1974

### Well H (Activated October 8, 1966)
- **Location:** East Woburn, 800 feet southeast of Well G
- **Depth:** 165 feet
- **Current Flow Rate:** 1,100 gallons per minute
- **Daily Pumping Volume:** 1.1 million gallons average
- **Operational Hours:** 16 hours daily average
- **Maintenance Status:** Pump motor servicing completed February 28, 1974

## WATER QUALITY MONITORING

Basic water quality testing has been conducted in accordance with existing municipal standards. Samples collected on March 5, 1974 from both wells indicate:

- **pH Levels:** Well G: 6.8, Well H: 7.1
- **Turbidity:** Both wells within acceptable limits
- **Chloride Content:** Well G: 42 mg/L, Well H: 38 mg/L
- **Iron Content:** Well G: 0.8 mg/L, Well H: 0.6 mg/L

All measurements fall within established parameters for municipal water supply as defined by current state regulations. No unusual odors, tastes, or discoloration have been reported by municipal workers or residents.

## DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM PERFORMANCE

The East Woburn distribution network served by Wells G and H continues to provide adequate water pressure and volume to approximately 1,847 residential units and 23 commercial establishments. Recent connections include:

- **Montvale Road Extension:** 12 new residential connections (January-March 1974)
- **Salem Street Commercial:** 2 light industrial connections (February 1974)
- **Pine Street Residential:** 8 new residential connections (March 1974)

Water pressure maintained at 45-50 PSI throughout the service area with no reported service interruptions during the reporting period.

## INDUSTRIAL COORDINATION

The Department continues to coordinate with local industrial facilities regarding water supply planning and infrastructure development. Recent communications with area businesses include:

- **W.R. Grace Company:** Ongoing discussions regarding expanded water service to manufacturing facilities
- **Cryovac Inc.:** Coordination on utility easements and service line routing
- **John J. Riley Company:** Water service planning for facility expansion

These industrial partnerships support economic development while ensuring adequate municipal water supply for residential customers.

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### Testing Protocols
Monthly bacterial testing conducted at both well sites with all samples showing negative results for coliform bacteria. Testing performed by Commonwealth Laboratory Services under contract with the City of Woburn.

### Documentation Requirements
All required operational logs maintained on-site at each well location. Daily pumping records, maintenance activities, and equipment inspections documented in accordance with DEQ guidelines.

### Reporting Schedule
This quarterly report fulfills current regulatory requirements. Next scheduled report due July 10, 1974, covering the period April 1 through June 30, 1974.

## INFRASTRUCTURE MAINTENANCE

### Completed Activities (January-March 1974)
- Well G: Replacement of primary electrical controls (January 18, 1974)
- Well H: Pump motor bearing replacement (February 28, 1974)
- Distribution System: Valve replacement on Salem Street main line (March 8, 1974)
- Both Wells: Quarterly safety equipment inspection (March 15, 1974)

### Scheduled Activities (April-June 1974)
- Well G: Annual pump efficiency testing (May 1974)
- Well H: Electrical system inspection (June 1974)
- Distribution System: Hydrant flushing program (April-May 1974)

## COMMUNITY IMPACT

Wells G and H continue to serve the growing East Woburn community effectively. Population growth in the service area has increased approximately 8% over the past year, with new residential construction concentrated along Montvale Road, Pine Street, and adjacent neighborhoods. The wells' combined capacity remains adequate to meet current and projected demand through 1975.

No citizen complaints regarding water quality, pressure, or service interruptions have been received during the reporting period. The Department maintains regular communication with residents through town meetings and responds promptly to any service concerns.

## ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS

Well sites are maintained in accordance with environmental protection standards. Immediate areas around both wells kept clear of debris and potential contamination sources. Security fencing and warning signage maintained in good condition.

The Department continues to monitor surrounding land use to ensure compatibility with municipal water supply operations. Industrial development in the area proceeds under appropriate oversight to maintain water quality standards.

## FUTURE PLANNING

### Capacity Analysis
Current combined well capacity of 2.3 million gallons per day exceeds present demand of approximately 2.0 million gallons per day, providing adequate reserve capacity for system reliability and future growth.

### Infrastructure Investment
Budget planning for fiscal year 1975 includes provisions for:
- Backup generator installation at Well G ($18,000)
- Distribution system expansion for new residential areas ($25,000)
- Enhanced monitoring equipment for both wells ($8,500)

## FINANCIAL SUMMARY

### Operating Costs (January-March 1974)
- Electricity: $3,247
- Maintenance and Repairs: $1,892
- Testing and Laboratory Services: $485
- Personnel (allocated): $4,200
- **Total Quarterly Operating Cost:** $9,824

### Revenue Generation
Water service fees collected during the reporting period totaled $28,450, maintaining positive operational cash flow and supporting continued system improvements.

## CONCLUSION

Wells G and H continue to operate effectively as essential components of the Woburn municipal water supply system. All regulatory requirements have been met during the reporting period, and the wells maintain their capacity to serve the East Woburn community reliably.

The Department remains committed to providing safe, adequate water service while supporting community growth and economic development. Regular monitoring and maintenance ensure continued compliance with all applicable state regulations.
