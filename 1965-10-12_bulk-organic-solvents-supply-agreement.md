## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Supplier is engaged in the business of distributing industrial chemicals and solvents to manufacturing operations throughout New England; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser operates a plastic film manufacturing facility in Woburn, Massachusetts, requiring regular supplies of organic solvents for production processes; and

WHEREAS, the parties desire to establish terms for the regular supply of bulk organic solvents to support Purchaser's manufacturing operations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I - PRODUCTS AND SPECIFICATIONS

**1.1 Products Covered**  
This Agreement covers the supply of the following organic solvents in bulk quantities:

- Trichloroethylene (TCE) - Technical Grade, minimum 99.5% purity
- Perchloroethylene (PCE) - Industrial Grade, minimum 99.0% purity  
- 1,1,1-Trichloroethane - Degreasing Grade, minimum 98.5% purity
- Methylene Chloride - Technical Grade, minimum 99.0% purity
- Carbon Tetrachloride - Industrial Grade, minimum 99.0% purity

**1.2 Delivery Specifications**  
All solvents shall be delivered in clean, dedicated tank trucks with capacities ranging from 3,000 to 6,000 gallons. Each delivery shall be accompanied by certificates of analysis confirming product specifications and purity levels.

**1.3 Quality Standards**  
All products shall meet or exceed American Chemical Society (ACS) specifications for industrial grade solvents and shall be suitable for use in plastic film manufacturing processes, including degreasing, cleaning, and solvent bonding operations.

## ARTICLE II - DELIVERY AND STORAGE

**2.1 Delivery Schedule**  
Supplier agrees to deliver solvents to Purchaser's facility at 15 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts, according to the following schedule:

- Trichloroethylene: Bi-weekly deliveries of 4,000-5,000 gallons
- Perchloroethylene: Monthly deliveries of 2,000-3,000 gallons
- Other solvents: As requested with minimum 48-hour notice

**2.2 Storage Responsibility**  
Purchaser shall provide adequate storage facilities including underground storage tanks with appropriate secondary containment systems. Purchaser represents that all storage facilities comply with applicable Massachusetts Department of Public Health regulations and local fire codes.

**2.3 Transfer Operations**  
All solvent transfers from delivery trucks to storage tanks shall be conducted using appropriate hoses, fittings, and containment procedures to prevent spillage or release to the environment. Purchaser's personnel shall supervise all transfer operations.

## ARTICLE III - PRICING AND PAYMENT TERMS

**3.1 Pricing**  
Pricing for each solvent shall be based on prevailing market rates at time of delivery, subject to the following base pricing structure effective as of the Agreement date:

- Trichloroethylene: $0.32 per gallon
- Perchloroethylene: $0.38 per gallon
- 1,1,1-Trichloroethane: $0.41 per gallon
- Methylene Chloride: $0.29 per gallon
- Carbon Tetrachloride: $0.35 per gallon

**3.2 Price Adjustments**  
Prices may be adjusted quarterly based on changes in raw material costs, transportation expenses, and market conditions, with thirty (30) days written notice to Purchaser.

**3.3 Payment Terms**  
Payment shall be net thirty (30) days from date of invoice. Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month.

## ARTICLE IV - RISK OF LOSS AND INSURANCE

**4.1 Risk of Loss**  
Risk of loss shall transfer to Purchaser upon delivery to Purchaser's storage facilities. Supplier shall maintain appropriate insurance coverage for transportation risks.

**4.2 Insurance Requirements**  
Each party shall maintain comprehensive general liability insurance with minimum coverage of $500,000 per occurrence. Purchaser shall maintain appropriate environmental impairment liability coverage for storage and handling operations.

## ARTICLE V - COMPLIANCE AND SAFETY

**5.1 Regulatory Compliance**  
Both parties acknowledge their respective obligations to comply with all applicable federal, state, and local regulations governing the transportation, storage, handling, and use of hazardous chemicals and solvents.

**5.2 Safety Protocols**  
Purchaser shall implement appropriate safety protocols for solvent handling, including proper ventilation systems, personal protective equipment for workers, and emergency response procedures.

**5.3 Waste Management**  
Purchaser shall be solely responsible for the proper management and disposal of any waste solvents, contaminated materials, or byproducts generated in connection with its manufacturing operations, in accordance with applicable environmental regulations.

## ARTICLE VI - WARRANTIES AND DISCLAIMERS

**6.1 Product Warranties**  
Supplier warrants that all products delivered hereunder shall conform to agreed specifications and shall be free from defects in materials. SUPPLIER MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

**6.2 Limitation of Liability**  
Supplier's liability for any claims arising under this Agreement shall be limited to replacement of non-conforming products or refund of amounts paid for such products.

## ARTICLE VII - INDEMNIFICATION

**7.1 Mutual Indemnification**  
Each party shall indemnify and hold harmless the other party from and against any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from such party's negligent performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

**7.2 Environmental Indemnification**  
Purchaser shall indemnify and hold harmless Supplier from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from Purchaser's storage, handling, use, or disposal of solvents delivered hereunder, including any environmental contamination or remediation costs.

## ARTICLE VIII - TERM AND TERMINATION

**8.1 Term**  
This Agreement shall commence on October 12, 1965, and shall continue for a period of two (2) years, automatically renewing for successive one-year periods unless terminated by either party with ninety (90) days written notice.

**8.2 Termination for Cause**  
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice in the event of material breach by the other party, subject to a thirty (30) day cure period.

## ARTICLE IX - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**9.1 Governing Law**  
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

**9.2 Entire Agreement**  
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

**9.3 Amendment**  
This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.
