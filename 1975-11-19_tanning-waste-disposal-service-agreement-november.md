# TANNING WASTE DISPOSAL SERVICE AGREEMENT

**COMPANY:** John J. Riley Company

**TELEPHONE:** (617) 933-2156  
**FEDERAL TAX ID:** 04-2847593  

**CONTRACTOR:** Industrial Waste Disposal Company  
**ADDRESS:** 485 Mystic Avenue, Medford, Massachusetts 02155  
**TELEPHONE:** (617) 396-7821  
**LICENSE NO.:** MA-IWD-847-75  

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company operates a leather tanning and finishing facility at the above-referenced address and generates various industrial wastes in the course of its manufacturing operations; and

WHEREAS, Contractor is duly licensed by the Commonwealth of Massachusetts to collect, transport, and dispose of industrial wastes; and

WHEREAS, Company requires regular and reliable disposal services for its tanning waste materials to maintain compliance with applicable regulations and ensure continuous operations; and

WHEREAS, the parties desire to establish the terms and conditions for waste disposal services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I - SCOPE OF SERVICES

**1.1 Waste Materials.** Contractor agrees to collect, transport, and dispose of the following waste materials generated by Company's tanning operations:

a) **Chromium-bearing sludges** from hide processing operations, estimated at 2,400-3,200 pounds per month
b) **Spent tanning solutions** containing chromium sulfate, estimated at 1,800-2,400 gallons per month  
c) **Dehairing and liming wastes** containing sulfides and lime, estimated at 1,200-1,600 pounds per month
d) **Organic solvent wastes** including trichloroethylene and perchloroethylene from degreasing operations, estimated at 240-360 gallons per month
e) **Leather finishing wastes** including dyes, lacquers, and cleaning solvents, estimated at 180-280 gallons per month

**1.2 Collection Schedule.** Contractor shall collect waste materials from Company's facility on a bi-weekly basis, specifically on alternate Wednesdays commencing December 3, 1975, between the hours of 8:00 AM and 4:00 PM, or as otherwise mutually agreed.

**1.3 Disposal Methods.** Contractor represents that all waste materials will be disposed of in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local regulations through approved disposal facilities. Contractor maintains disposal arrangements with the following facilities:

a) **Secure Chemical Services, Inc.** - Bridgeport, Connecticut (EPA Permit No. CT-0847-75)
b) **Industrial Recovery Corporation** - Fall River, Massachusetts (State Permit No. MA-IRC-284-74)
c) **Metro Waste Management** - Everett, Massachusetts (Municipal Permit No. EV-MWM-156-75)

## ARTICLE II - COMPANY OBLIGATIONS

**2.1 Waste Preparation.** Company shall segregate and containerize all waste materials according to Contractor's specifications, including:

a) Use of approved 55-gallon steel drums for liquid wastes
b) Use of approved fiber drums or steel containers for solid wastes
c) Proper labeling of all containers with contents and hazard classifications
d) Maintenance of waste manifests documenting quantities and compositions

**2.2 Storage Requirements.** Company shall maintain adequate storage areas for waste materials pending collection, ensuring containers remain sealed and secure from weather and unauthorized access.

**2.3 Access and Safety.** Company shall provide Contractor reasonable access to waste storage areas and shall notify Contractor of any special safety precautions or handling requirements for specific waste materials.

## ARTICLE III - CONTRACTOR OBLIGATIONS

**3.1 Professional Standards.** Contractor shall perform all services in accordance with industry standards and applicable regulations, maintaining all required licenses and permits in good standing.

**3.2 Transportation.** Contractor shall transport all waste materials using properly licensed and equipped vehicles with appropriate placarding and documentation as required by Department of Transportation regulations.

**3.3 Documentation.** Contractor shall provide Company with copies of all disposal receipts, manifests, and certificates of destruction or treatment within thirty (30) days of disposal.

**3.4 Insurance.** Contractor shall maintain comprehensive general liability insurance of not less than $500,000 and environmental impairment liability coverage of not less than $1,000,000, naming Company as additional insured.

## ARTICLE IV - COMPENSATION

**4.1 Service Fees.** Company agrees to pay Contractor the following monthly fees:

a) **Base collection fee:** $485.00 per month
b) **Chromium waste disposal:** $0.78 per pound
c) **Solvent waste disposal:** $2.15 per gallon
d) **General industrial waste:** $0.42 per pound
e) **Transportation surcharge:** $125.00 per month

**4.2 Payment Terms.** Contractor shall invoice Company monthly, with payment due within thirty (30) days of invoice date. Late payments shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

**4.3 Price Adjustments.** Contractor may adjust disposal fees annually upon sixty (60) days written notice to reflect changes in disposal costs or regulatory requirements.

## ARTICLE V - REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

**5.1 Permits and Approvals.** Each party represents that it maintains all permits, licenses, and approvals necessary for the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

**5.2 Regulatory Changes.** In the event of changes to applicable environmental regulations affecting waste disposal requirements, the parties agree to negotiate in good faith any necessary modifications to this Agreement.

**5.3 Compliance Certification.** Contractor certifies that all disposal methods comply with applicable regulations and that disposal facilities are properly licensed and operated in accordance with environmental standards.

## ARTICLE VI - TERM AND TERMINATION

**6.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on December 1, 1975, and continue for a period of twenty-four (24) months, unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions hereof.

**6.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice in the event of material breach by the other party, provided such breach is not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice.

**6.3 Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party.

## ARTICLE VII - INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

**7.1 Contractor Indemnification.** Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company from and against any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from Contractor's negligent performance of services hereunder, including but not limited to improper disposal, transportation accidents, or regulatory violations.

**7.2 Company Indemnification.** Company agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Contractor from and against any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from the nature or composition of waste materials provided by Company, provided such materials are properly disclosed and documented.

**7.3 Limitation of Liability.** Neither party shall be liable for consequential, incidental, or punitive damages arising from performance under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE VIII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**8.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

**8.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

**8.3 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.

**8.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is deemed invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**8.5 Force Majeure.** Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, government actions, or labor disputes.
