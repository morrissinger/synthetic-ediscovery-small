# EMERGENCY MEETING MINUTES
## CONTAMINATED WELLS G AND H RESPONSE

**Date:** May 9, 1979  
**Time:** 8:45 AM  
**Location:** Woburn City Hall, Conference Room B  
**Called by:** Dr. Margaret Sullivan, City Health Officer  

### ATTENDEES

**Present:**
- Dr. Margaret Sullivan, City Health Officer (Chair)
- Robert McKenzie, Public Works Director
- Thomas Hartley, Water Superintendent
- Frank DiMarco, Mayor's Office Representative
- Patricia O'Brien, City Attorney
- James Mitchell, Environmental Consultant

**Absent:**
- Mayor John Curran (out of state)

### AGENDA ITEM 1: IMMEDIATE WELL CLOSURE

Dr. Sullivan opened the emergency session at 8:45 AM, stating that preliminary test results from Wells G and H showed alarming levels of volatile organic compounds, specifically trichloroethylene (TCE) at concentrations exceeding 267 parts per billion in Well G and 183 parts per billion in Well H.

**Discussion Points:**
- Water Superintendent Hartley reported that both wells were immediately taken offline at 6:30 AM pending further analysis
- McKenzie confirmed backup water sources from Wells A, B, and C can maintain adequate supply to affected neighborhoods
- Dr. Sullivan emphasized the urgent public health implications, noting these contamination levels far exceed any acceptable standards

**DECISION:** Wells G and H to remain closed indefinitely pending comprehensive contamination assessment.

**ACTION ITEMS:**
- Hartley to coordinate with Massachusetts Water Resources Authority for emergency water supply protocols (Due: May 10)
- McKenzie to prepare public notification system for affected residents (Due: May 11)

### AGENDA ITEM 2: SOURCE INVESTIGATION AND INDUSTRIAL FACILITIES

Dr. Sullivan presented preliminary findings suggesting industrial contamination sources northeast and southwest of the well sites.

**Discussion Points:**
- Environmental Consultant Mitchell identified W.R. Grace Company's Cryovac facility at 369 Washington Street as potential source northeast of wells
- Historical solvent disposal practices at the Grace property documented through employee interviews
- Beatrice Foods' Riley tannery operation at 228 Salem Street identified as secondary potential source
- City Attorney O'Brien advised immediate documentation of all evidence for potential legal proceedings

**DECISION:** Initiate comprehensive environmental investigation of both industrial sites.

**ACTION ITEMS:**
- Mitchell to conduct soil and groundwater sampling around Grace and Beatrice properties (Due: May 15)
- O'Brien to research municipal authority for emergency environmental inspections (Due: May 12)
- Dr. Sullivan to coordinate with Massachusetts Department of Public Health for expanded testing (Due: May 14)

### AGENDA ITEM 3: PUBLIC HEALTH ASSESSMENT

Dr. Sullivan reported concerning health patterns in East Woburn neighborhoods served by Wells G and H.

**Discussion Points:**
- Preliminary review indicates elevated childhood leukemia cases in areas served by contaminated wells
- Families including the Andersons, Kanes, Zonas, Toomeys, Robbins, and Gamaches identified for immediate health screening
- Wells G and H provided water to approximately 5,000 residents since 1964 and 1966 respectively
- Dr. Sullivan emphasized city's duty of care to residents who consumed contaminated water unknowingly

**DECISION:** Establish immediate health monitoring program for affected residents.

**ACTION ITEMS:**
- Dr. Sullivan to contact CDC for epidemiological support (Due: May 16)
- DiMarco to coordinate with Mayor's office for emergency health services funding (Due: May 13)
- O'Brien to review municipal liability exposure and insurance coverage (Due: May 15)

### AGENDA ITEM 4: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND REPORTING

City Attorney O'Brien outlined mandatory reporting requirements to state and federal agencies.

**Discussion Points:**
- EPA notification required within 24 hours of contamination discovery
- Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering must be notified immediately
- State Department of Public Health requires comprehensive health impact assessment
- Federal Safe Drinking Water Act compliance issues require immediate attention

**DECISION:** Full regulatory disclosure and cooperation with all investigating agencies.

**ACTION ITEMS:**
- O'Brien to file required notifications with EPA and state agencies (Due: May 10)
- Dr. Sullivan to prepare comprehensive health officer's report (Due: May 18)
- McKenzie to compile complete operational history of Wells G and H (Due: May 20)

### AGENDA ITEM 5: CORPORATE ACCOUNTABILITY AND LEGAL STRATEGY

City Attorney O'Brien addressed potential legal remedies against responsible industrial parties.

**Discussion Points:**
- W.R. Grace Company's documented solvent use and waste disposal practices establish potential negligence liability
- Beatrice Foods' tannery operations involved chemical processes that may have contributed to groundwater contamination
- Municipal duty of care to residents may have been breached through inadequate water quality monitoring
- Environmental damage assessment required for potential cost recovery litigation

**DECISION:** Preserve all evidence and prepare for potential legal action against responsible parties.

**ACTION ITEMS:**
- O'Brien to engage specialized environmental litigation counsel (Due: May 25)
- All department heads to preserve documents related to Wells G and H operations (Ongoing)
- Mitchell to prepare comprehensive contamination source analysis (Due: May 30)

### ADDITIONAL BUSINESS

Dr. Sullivan emphasized the gravity of the situation, noting that families had consumed contaminated water for up to fifteen years without knowledge of the health risks. The meeting concluded with unanimous agreement that the city must take all necessary steps to protect public health while pursuing accountability from responsible industrial parties.

**Next Meeting:** May 16, 1979, 2:00 PM, same location

**Meeting Adjourned:** 10:15 AM
