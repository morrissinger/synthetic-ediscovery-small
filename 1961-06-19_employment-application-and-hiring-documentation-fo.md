### JOB RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

**Primary Functions:**
1. Operation of packaging machinery for food preservation materials
2. Handling and mixing of chemical solutions used in film production
3. Quality control inspection of finished plastic film products
4. Maintenance of production equipment and work area cleanliness
5. Loading and unloading of raw materials and finished goods

**Chemical Handling Requirements:**
- Work with trichloroethylene (TCE) cleaning solutions
- Handle perchloroethylene (PCE) in degreasing operations
- Mix adhesive compounds containing various industrial solvents
- Operate vapor degreasing equipment using chlorinated compounds

**Safety Equipment Provided:**
- Work gloves (rubber and leather)
- Safety glasses
- Protective aprons
- First aid supplies in work area

### COMPANY POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

**Work Schedule:**
Regular work week consists of five (5) eight-hour days, Monday through Friday. Overtime work may be required during peak production periods. All employees are expected to maintain punctual attendance and notify their supervisor of any absence.

**Wage and Benefits:**
Starting wage of $1.85 per hour with opportunity for advancement based on performance and length of service. Company provides group health insurance after 90 days of employment. Paid vacation of one week after one year of service, two weeks after five years.

**Safety Regulations:**
1. All employees must follow established safety procedures at all times
2. Report any accidents or injuries immediately to supervisor
3. Use provided safety equipment when handling chemicals
4. No smoking in production areas
5. Maintain clean and organized work stations

**Waste Disposal Procedures:**
Chemical waste materials are to be disposed of according to company guidelines. Used solvents and cleaning solutions should be collected in designated containers for proper disposal. Under no circumstances should chemical waste be poured down drains or disposed of on company property without authorization from the Production Foreman.

### EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

**In Case of Chemical Exposure:**
1. Remove affected person from exposure area immediately
2. Flush affected skin or eyes with clean water for 15 minutes
3. Notify supervisor and call company nurse
4. Seek medical attention if symptoms persist
