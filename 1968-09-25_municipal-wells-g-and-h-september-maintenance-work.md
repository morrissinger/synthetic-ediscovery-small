## SCHEDULED MAINTENANCE ACTIVITIES

### Well G Maintenance Requirements

**1. Pump System Inspection**
- Visual inspection of pump housing and electrical connections
- Check amperage draw and voltage readings
- Inspect pump discharge line for corrosion or blockage
- Test automatic start/stop controls
- Verify proper operation of pressure switch settings (cut-in 40 PSI, cut-out 60 PSI)

**2. Water Quality Testing**
- Collect samples for bacteriological analysis per state health department requirements
- Conduct field testing for chlorine residual levels
- Check pH levels and record readings
- Visual inspection for turbidity, color, or unusual odor
- Submit samples to Massachusetts Department of Public Health laboratory if abnormalities detected

**3. Well Head Inspection**
- Inspect concrete well house for structural integrity
- Check security of access covers and ventilation systems
- Verify proper grading around well head to prevent surface water infiltration
- Inspect electrical panel and control systems
- Test emergency shutdown procedures

### Well H Maintenance Requirements

**1. Pump System Inspection**
- Complete pump performance evaluation including flow rate testing
- Inspect pump motor bearings and electrical connections
- Check discharge piping and valve operations
- Test water level controls and float switches
- Verify proper operation of pressure tank systems

**2. Water Distribution System**
- Inspect connection to main distribution line serving East Woburn residential area
- Check pressure reducing valve settings
- Test backflow prevention devices
- Verify proper chlorination injection system operation
- Inspect meter readings and flow measurement devices

**3. System Integration Testing**
- Test coordinated operation between Wells G and H
- Verify proper sequencing of pump operations during peak demand periods
- Check emergency backup procedures
- Test communication systems between well sites and central control

## WATER QUALITY MONITORING PROTOCOLS

### Routine Testing Schedule
- **Daily:** Visual inspection, chlorine residual testing
- **Weekly:** pH and turbidity measurements
- **Monthly:** Bacteriological sampling as required by state regulations
- **Quarterly:** Comprehensive water quality analysis including mineral content

### Sample Collection Procedures
1. Allow pump to operate for minimum 15 minutes before sampling
2. Disinfect sample tap with alcohol solution
3. Collect samples in sterile containers provided by state laboratory
4. Label samples with well identification, date, time, and collector name
5. Maintain chain of custody documentation
6. Transport samples to Massachusetts Department of Public Health laboratory within 24 hours

### Documentation Requirements
- Record all test results in official Water Quality Log Book
- Report any abnormal readings to Water Department Supervisor immediately
- File monthly summary reports with Department of Public Works
- Maintain copies of all laboratory analysis reports

---

## SAFETY AND OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES

### Personnel Safety Requirements
- All maintenance personnel must be certified in confined space entry procedures
- Use of proper ventilation equipment required for well house entry
- Electrical lockout/tagout procedures must be followed during pump maintenance
- Emergency contact information posted at each well site

### Equipment and Materials Needed
- Standard electrical testing equipment (multimeter, amp clamp)
- Water sampling kit with sterile containers
- pH testing kit and chlorine test strips
- Basic hand tools for mechanical inspections
- Emergency repair supplies (fuses, gaskets, minor electrical components)

### Emergency Procedures
- In case of pump failure: Immediately notify Water Department central control
- For water quality concerns: Stop well operation and contact health department
- Equipment malfunction: Follow standard lockout procedures and request repair authorization
- After-hours emergencies: Contact on-call supervisor at MA-4-7829
