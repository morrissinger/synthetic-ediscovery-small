# JOHN J. RILEY COMPANY
## LEATHER PROCESSING OPERATIONS
### EMPLOYEE TRAINING RECORD

**Date:** June 7, 1921  
**Time:** 4:00 PM  
**Location:** John J. Riley Tannery, 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts  
**Supervisor:** John J. Riley, Owner-Manager

## TRAINING COMPLETION CERTIFICATION

This document certifies that **William J. Riley Jr.** has completed comprehensive training in leather processing operations at the John J. Riley Company tannery facility. Training commenced on May 15, 1921, and concluded on June 7, 1921, encompassing twenty-four working days of intensive instruction in all aspects of hide processing, chemical handling, and facility management.

### TRAINING MODULES COMPLETED

#### **Module I: Raw Hide Reception and Preparation (May 15-18, 1921)**
Young Riley demonstrated competency in:
- Inspection of incoming cattle hides from Boston stockyards
- Proper storage techniques in salt preservation rooms
- Initial cleaning and preparation procedures
- Quality grading system implementation

**Performance Rating:** Satisfactory  
**Supervisor Notes:** Shows natural aptitude for leather assessment. Requires continued practice in hide grading consistency.

#### **Module II: Chemical Processing and Tanning Operations (May 19-28, 1921)**
Trainee successfully completed instruction in:
- Lime liquor preparation and application for hair removal
- Chromium sulfate tanning solution mixing and monitoring
- Proper handling of sulfuric acid for pH adjustment
- Vegetable tanning extract preparation using oak bark and hemlock
- Deliming processes using ammonium sulfate solutions

**Performance Rating:** Good  
**Supervisor Notes:** Demonstrates careful attention to chemical proportions. Maintains proper safety protocols when handling acid solutions. Natural understanding of pH balance requirements for quality leather production.

#### **Module III: Mechanical Processing Equipment (May 29-June 2, 1921)**
Training encompassed operation of:
- Steam-powered hide splitting machines
- Mechanical fleshing equipment for subcutaneous fat removal
- Hydraulic pressing systems for moisture extraction
- Belt-driven buffing and finishing machinery
- Boiler operation and maintenance for steam generation

**Performance Rating:** Excellent  
**Supervisor Notes:** Shows mechanical aptitude inherited from Riley family tradition. Maintains equipment properly and understands maintenance scheduling requirements.

#### **Module IV: Waste Management and Facility Operations (June 3-7, 1921)**
Final training module covered:
- Proper disposal methods for organic waste materials
- Chemical waste handling and storage procedures
- Maintenance of settling ponds for processing water
- Coordination with municipal waste removal services
- Record keeping for chemical inventory and usage

**Performance Rating:** Satisfactory  
**Supervisor Notes:** Understands importance of efficient waste disposal for operational continuity. Requires additional instruction in chemical inventory management systems.

### CHEMICAL HANDLING CERTIFICATIONS

William J. Riley Jr. has demonstrated competency in safe handling of the following industrial chemicals used in leather processing operations:

- **Chromium Sulfate Solutions** (Cr₂(SO₄)₃): Proper mixing ratios, pH monitoring, worker safety protocols
- **Sulfuric Acid** (H₂SO₄): Dilution procedures, personal protective equipment usage, spill response
- **Lime Solutions** (Ca(OH)₂): Preparation of lime liquors, application techniques, neutralization methods
- **Ammonium Sulfate** ((NH₄)₂SO₄): Deliming applications, proper storage, handling precautions
- **Sodium Chloride Brines**: Hide preservation, salt storage, contamination prevention

### FACILITY MANAGEMENT RESPONSIBILITIES

Upon completion of this training program, William J. Riley Jr. is authorized to assume the following operational responsibilities:

1. **Daily Production Oversight**: Supervision of hide processing from reception through finished leather production
2. **Chemical Inventory Management**: Ordering, receiving, and proper storage of all processing chemicals
3. **Quality Control**: Implementation of Riley Company standards for leather grade certification
4. **Worker Safety Coordination**: Ensuring proper safety protocols in chemical handling areas
5. **Waste Disposal Supervision**: Oversight of all waste materials removal from facility premises

### BUSINESS SUCCESSION PREPARATION

As the designated successor to John J. Riley Company operations, William J. Riley Jr. has received additional instruction in:

- **Customer Relations**: Maintaining relationships with Boston-area leather goods manufacturers
- **Supplier Negotiations**: Procurement of raw hides and chemical supplies at competitive pricing
- **Financial Management**: Cost accounting for chemical usage, labor expenses, and facility maintenance
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Understanding of Massachusetts industrial operation requirements
- **Facility Expansion Planning**: Preparation for potential tannery capacity increases

After twenty-four days of comprehensive training, I find William J. Riley Jr. well-prepared to assume increasing responsibilities in tannery operations. His natural aptitude for the leather processing trade, combined with careful attention to chemical handling procedures, positions him to maintain the Riley Company's reputation for quality leather production.
