### DISCHARGE PLANNING AND INSTRUCTIONS

**Feeding:**
- Breastfeeding encouraged and initiated successfully
- Mother demonstrates proper latching technique
- If supplemental feeding becomes necessary, recommend Similac or Enfamil infant formula
- Feed every 2-3 hours or on demand

**Care Instructions:**
- Keep umbilical cord area clean and dry
- Sponge baths only until cord falls off (typically 7-14 days)
- Contact physician if cord becomes red, swollen, or has foul odor
- Normal newborn bowel movements vary from yellow to green, may be loose

**Warning Signs - Contact Physician Immediately:**
- Fever greater than 100.4°F (rectal)
- Difficulty breathing or persistent cough
- Refusal to eat for more than 6 hours
- Excessive vomiting or diarrhea
- Unusual lethargy or difficulty waking
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes) that worsens or persists beyond 2 weeks
