## CONTRACTOR CERTIFICATION

Industrial Waste Management Services Inc., a Massachusetts licensed waste disposal contractor (License No. MA-WD-4471, issued June 15, 1973, expires June 14, 1976), hereby certifies completion of waste disposal services for W.R. Grace Company's Woburn manufacturing facility located at 62 Whittemore Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts, in accordance with the terms of Service Contract No. WG-7611-042 dated October 12, 1976.

This certification is submitted in compliance with Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 21C and regulations promulgated thereunder by the Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering, specifically 310 CMR 30.000 et seq., governing the transportation, treatment, and disposal of industrial waste materials.

## WASTE CHARACTERIZATION AND QUANTITIES

The following industrial waste materials were collected, transported, and disposed of during the period November 1-20, 1976:

### Chemical Solvents and Process Residues
- **Trichloroethylene (TCE)**: 2,840 gallons in 55-gallon drums
- **Tetrachloroethylene (PCE)**: 1,650 gallons in 55-gallon drums
- **1,1,1-Trichloroethane**: 980 gallons in 55-gallon drums
- **Methylene chloride**: 1,200 gallons in 55-gallon drums
- **Mixed chlorinated solvents**: 3,200 gallons in various containers

### Contaminated Materials
- **Solvent-soaked rags and absorbent materials**: 14 cubic yards
- **Contaminated equipment and piping**: 8 cubic yards
- **Sludge from degreasing operations**: 1,840 gallons
- **Filter media and activated carbon**: 2.4 tons

### Metal Finishing Wastes
- **Chromium-containing solutions**: 680 gallons
- **Electroplating bath residues**: 420 gallons
- **Metal hydroxide sludges**: 1.2 tons

**Total Volume Disposed:** Approximately 11,790 gallons of liquid waste and 26.4 cubic yards of solid waste materials.

## DISPOSAL METHODOLOGY AND LOCATIONS

All waste materials were transported in accordance with Department of Transportation regulations under 49 CFR Parts 100-199 and disposed of at the following approved facilities:

### Primary Disposal Site
**Facility:** Secure Chemical Waste Management Facility  
**Location:** Route 1A, Tyngsborough, Massachusetts  
**Permit No.:** DEQE-SWM-1974-018  
**Method:** Secure landfill disposal with clay liner and leachate collection system

### Secondary Disposal Site
**Facility:** Industrial Recovery Corporation  
**Location:** Nashua Road, Billerica, Massachusetts  
**Permit No.:** DEQE-SWM-1975-031  
**Method:** Incineration at 2,000Â°F minimum temperature with air pollution control equipment

All liquid chlorinated solvents were disposed of through high-temperature incineration to ensure complete destruction of organic compounds. Solid wastes and sludges were placed in secure landfill cells designed to prevent groundwater contamination through engineered containment systems.

## TRANSPORTATION DOCUMENTATION

Waste transportation was conducted using vehicles properly placarded in accordance with DOT Hazardous Materials Regulations. All drivers possessed valid Commercial Driver's Licenses with hazardous materials endorsements. The following vehicles were utilized:

- **Vehicle 1:** 1975 Peterbilt Model 359 with 4,000-gallon tanker trailer
- **Vehicle 2:** 1974 International Harvester with 20-cubic-yard roll-off container
- **Vehicle 3:** 1976 Ford F-700 with specialized drum handling equipment

Each shipment was accompanied by properly completed Uniform Hazardous Waste Manifests as required under developing federal regulations and existing state requirements. Manifest copies have been retained in our files and are available for regulatory inspection upon request.

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATION

Industrial Waste Management Services Inc. certifies that all waste disposal activities were conducted in full compliance with applicable federal, state, and local regulations, including:

1. **Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 21C** - Hazardous Waste Management Act
2. **310 CMR 30.000** - Massachusetts Hazardous Waste Regulations  
3. **49 CFR Parts 100-199** - DOT Hazardous Materials Transportation Regulations
4. **Local ordinances** of the municipalities through which waste was transported

All disposal facilities utilized possess valid operating permits from the Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering and maintain appropriate insurance coverage and financial assurance mechanisms as required by law.

## CHAIN OF CUSTODY DOCUMENTATION

Proper chain of custody was maintained throughout the waste handling process:

1. **Initial Custody:** W.R. Grace Company environmental personnel supervised waste containerization and labeling
2. **Transfer to Contractor:** Industrial Waste Management Services assumed custody upon signature of Bill of Lading dated November 1, 1976
3. **Transportation:** Licensed drivers maintained custody during transport with continuous vehicle monitoring
4. **Final Disposal:** Receiving facilities signed acceptance documentation confirming proper disposal

All custody transfer documentation has been retained and filed in accordance with regulatory requirements for a minimum retention period of three years.

## ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING AND VERIFICATION

Post-disposal monitoring activities have been initiated at both disposal facilities to verify the effectiveness of containment systems:

### Groundwater Monitoring
- **Monitoring wells:** Installed upgradient and downgradient of disposal areas
- **Sampling frequency:** Quarterly analysis for volatile organic compounds
- **Parameters monitored:** TCE, PCE, 1,1,1-TCA, methylene chloride, and other chlorinated solvents
- **Detection limits:** 5 parts per billion or lower for each monitored compound

### Leachate Analysis
- **Collection system monitoring:** Monthly volume measurements and chemical analysis
- **Treatment verification:** Confirmation that leachate treatment systems achieve required effluent standards
- **Reporting:** Quarterly reports submitted to DEQE as required by facility permits
