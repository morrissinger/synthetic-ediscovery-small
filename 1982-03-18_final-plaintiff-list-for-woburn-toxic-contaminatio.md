### III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS COMMON TO ALL COUNTS

**A. Water Supply System**

13. The City of Woburn operates a municipal water supply system that has served residents of East Woburn since the 1960s through Wells G and H.

14. Well G was constructed and placed into service in 1964, located approximately 1,500 feet northeast of the Cryovac facility.

15. Well H was constructed and placed into service in 1966, located approximately 2,000 feet east of the John J. Riley tanning facility.

16. Both wells drew water from the same underground aquifer system that extends beneath the industrial properties operated by Defendants.

**B. Industrial Operations and Chemical Disposal**

17. From approximately 1960 through 1979, Defendant Cryovac Inc. operated a plastic manufacturing facility at 61 Olympia Avenue, using substantial quantities of trichloroethylene (TCE) and tetrachloroethylene (PCE) as industrial solvents.

18. Defendant John J. Riley Company operated a leather tanning facility at 38 Salem Street from approximately 1945 through 1980, utilizing various chemical solvents including TCE in its tanning operations.

19. Upon information and belief, Defendants disposed of waste solvents and chemical byproducts by dumping, burying, or otherwise releasing them onto their respective properties without adequate containment or treatment.
