## MORNING OPERATIONS SUMMARY

**6:00 AM - Shift Start**
- Arrived on site to begin morning inspection of tanning operations
- Checked chemical storage tanks - chromium sulfate levels adequate for day's production
- Noted slight discoloration in drainage area near eastern property boundary
- Temperature readings normal across all processing vats

**7:15 AM - Production Line Inspection**
- Hide processing proceeding normally in Vats 1-6
- Vat 3 showing signs of overflow from yesterday's heavy production run
- Instructed crew to increase drainage pumping to prevent spillage onto floor
- Chemical mixture ratios maintained per standard operating procedures

**8:30 AM - Waste Disposal Activities**
- Morning waste disposal commenced as scheduled
- Approximately 450 gallons of spent tanning liquor pumped from settling tanks
- Chromium-bearing waste directed to eastern drainage system per established protocol
- Sludge accumulation in holding pond appears heavier than typical - likely due to increased production volume this week

**9:45 AM - Equipment Maintenance**
- Inspected drainage pipes leading toward Aberjona River boundary
- Found minor leak in junction pipe approximately 200 feet from main processing building
- Temporary repair applied with metal clamps and rubber gaskets
- Noted for permanent repair during next scheduled maintenance period

## CHEMICAL INVENTORY AND USAGE

**Chromium Compounds:**
- Chromium sulfate: 2,200 lbs consumed
- Chromic acid solution: 180 gallons processed
- Basic chromium sulfate: 340 lbs utilized

**Organic Solvents:**
- Trichloroethylene: 85 gallons used for degreasing operations
- Perchloroethylene: 62 gallons consumed in cleaning processes
- Benzene compounds: 45 gallons utilized for leather conditioning

**Disposal Methods:**
- Liquid wastes directed to eastern drainage system
- Solid chromium sludge deposited in designated holding area
- Organic solvent residues combined with general liquid waste stream
- No special handling protocols required per current company procedures

## ENVIRONMENTAL OBSERVATIONS

**Ground Conditions:**
- Soil around eastern drainage outlets showing characteristic blue-green staining
- Some vegetation discoloration noted along drainage path extending toward river boundary
- Ground moisture levels elevated in disposal areas due to recent heavy production

**Water Discharge:**
- Discharge from main outfall pipe flowing normally toward Aberjona River
- Water color slightly darker than usual - consistent with increased chromium content
- No unusual odors detected beyond normal tanning operation smells

**Wildlife Activity:**
- Fewer birds observed in immediate vicinity of disposal areas
- Small animals appear to avoid eastern drainage zones
- Fish activity in nearby Aberjona River sections appears normal
