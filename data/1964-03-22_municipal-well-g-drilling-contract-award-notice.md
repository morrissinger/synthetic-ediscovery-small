This letter serves as formal notification that the City of Woburn Department of Public Works has awarded Contract No. DPW-64-007 for the drilling and installation of Municipal Well G to **Consolidated Well Drilling Corporation** in the amount of **$18,750.00**.

## Contract Terms and Specifications

**WHEREAS**, the City of Woburn requires expansion of municipal water supply capacity to serve the growing population of approximately 35,000 residents; and

**WHEREAS**, Consolidated Well Drilling Corporation submitted the lowest qualified bid meeting all technical specifications as outlined in Request for Proposals DPW-RFP-64-003 dated February 15, 1964; and

**WHEREAS**, the proposed well site has been surveyed and approved by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health under permit MPH-64-WS-127;

**NOW THEREFORE**, the parties agree to the following terms and conditions:

### Section 1: Scope of Work

The Contractor shall provide all labor, materials, equipment, and services necessary for the complete drilling, installation, and testing of Municipal Well G, including but not limited to:

1. **Site Preparation**: Clearing and grading of designated well site located at coordinates 42°29'15"N, 71°09'42"W, approximately 200 feet east of the Aberjona River in the East Woburn section of the city.

2. **Well Drilling**: Drilling of water well to a minimum depth of 85 feet and maximum depth of 95 feet, or until adequate water-bearing formation is encountered, whichever occurs first. Drilling shall utilize rotary drilling method with 8-inch diameter casing.

3. **Casing Installation**: Installation of steel well casing conforming to American Water Works Association standards, with proper grouting and sealing to prevent surface contamination infiltration.

4. **Pump Installation**: Installation of submersible pump system with capacity of 400 gallons per minute, including electrical connections and control systems.

5. **Testing and Certification**: Conduct pumping tests for minimum 72-hour period to verify yield capacity and water quality sampling as required by Massachusetts Department of Public Health regulations.

### Section 2: Technical Specifications

The well shall be constructed to tap the Aberjona River aquifer system, which geological surveys indicate contains adequate water supply for municipal use. The contractor shall:

- Maintain detailed drilling logs documenting soil and rock formations encountered
- Install sanitary well head protection conforming to state health department standards  
- Ensure all materials meet or exceed American Water Works Association specifications
- Provide warranty on all equipment and installation for period of two (2) years

### Section 3: Water Quality Requirements

All water produced by Well G must meet Massachusetts Department of Public Health drinking water standards as established in regulation 105 CMR 410.000. Initial water quality testing shall include analysis for:

- Bacterial contamination (coliform testing)
- Chemical analysis including pH, hardness, iron, and manganese
- Turbidity and color measurements
- Any additional testing required by state health authorities

### Section 4: Timeline and Completion

Work shall commence no later than **April 15, 1964** and be completed by **June 30, 1964**. Time is of the essence due to anticipated increased water demand during summer months. Contractor shall provide weekly progress reports to the Department of Public Works.

### Section 5: Payment Terms

Total contract amount of $18,750.00 shall be paid as follows:
- 25% upon commencement of drilling operations
- 50% upon completion of well installation and casing
- 25% upon successful completion of all testing and final acceptance

### Section 6: Insurance and Bonding

Contractor shall maintain comprehensive general liability insurance in the amount of $50,000 and shall provide performance bond equal to 100% of contract value through qualified surety company.

### Section 7: Regulatory Compliance

All work shall be performed in accordance with:
- Massachusetts Department of Public Health regulations for public water supply wells
- City of Woburn building and zoning ordinances
- Occupational Safety and Health requirements
- All applicable federal, state, and local environmental regulations

## Authorization and Acceptance

This contract award is authorized by vote of the Woburn Board of Public Works on March 18, 1964, and is contingent upon:

1. Receipt of executed contract documents within ten (10) days
2. Provision of required insurance certificates and performance bond
3. Final approval of well site by Massachusetts Department of Public Health
4. Availability of appropriated funds as approved by City Council

The City of Woburn looks forward to the successful completion of this important infrastructure project, which will enhance our ability to provide safe, reliable water service to residents of East Woburn and support continued community growth and development.
