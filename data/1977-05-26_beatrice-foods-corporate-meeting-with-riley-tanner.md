# MEETING MINUTES
## Beatrice Foods Corporate Meeting with Riley Tannery Management

**Date:** May 26, 1977  
**Time:** 11:45 AM - 1:15 PM  
**Location:** Riley Tannery Plant Office, 15 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts  
**Meeting Type:** Quarterly Operations Review and Environmental Compliance Discussion

### ATTENDEES

**Beatrice Foods Corporate Representatives:**
- Robert Harrison, Vice President of Operations
- Margaret Sullivan, Environmental Affairs Director
- Thomas Mitchell, Legal Counsel

**Riley Tannery Management:**
- William J. McNeil, Plant Manager (Meeting Chair)
- David Kowalski, Production Supervisor
- Patricia Reynolds, Quality Control Manager

### AGENDA ITEMS DISCUSSED

#### 1. PRODUCTION OPERATIONS REVIEW

Plant Manager McNeil reported current production levels at 85% capacity, with leather processing operations maintaining consistent output despite recent equipment maintenance issues. The chromium tanning process continues to operate within established parameters, though chemical inventory management requires ongoing attention due to supply chain fluctuations affecting both chromium sulfate and sodium dichromate procurement.

**Discussion Points:**
- Monthly production targets remain achievable through Q3 1977
- Equipment replacement schedule for aging tanning drums approved for FY1978
- Raw hide quality from Midwest suppliers showing improvement

**Decisions Made:**
- Continue current production schedule
- Authorize $45,000 equipment upgrade for October installation
- Implement enhanced quality control protocols for incoming materials

#### 2. WASTE MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES

Production Supervisor Kowalski provided detailed overview of current waste disposal practices, emphasizing adherence to established protocols for chromium-containing waste streams. The facility continues to utilize on-site lagoon systems for initial treatment of tanning liquors, with periodic discharge to municipal sewer systems following preliminary processing.

**Key Operational Details:**
- Daily waste generation averaging 2,800 gallons of process water containing chromium compounds
- Lagoon capacity sufficient for current operations with periodic overflow management
- Sludge removal conducted quarterly by contracted waste management services

**Discussion Points:**
- Recent inquiries from Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering regarding discharge permits
- Neighboring property owners' complaints about odors during summer months
- Cost implications of enhanced treatment requirements under consideration by state regulators

**Decisions Made:**
- Continue current waste management protocols pending regulatory clarification
- Schedule meeting with DEQE representatives for June 15, 1977
- Implement additional lime treatment to address odor concerns

#### 3. ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE STATUS

Environmental Affairs Director Sullivan reviewed recent regulatory developments affecting leather processing operations, noting increased scrutiny from both state and federal agencies regarding industrial discharge standards. The discussion focused on potential impacts of proposed EPA guidelines for leather tanning facilities and their implications for current operations.

**Regulatory Concerns Addressed:**
- Proposed chromium discharge limits under Clean Water Act amendments
- Groundwater monitoring requirements for facilities with on-site disposal systems
- Record-keeping obligations for hazardous waste generation and disposal

**Discussion Points:**
- Legal Counsel Mitchell emphasized importance of maintaining comprehensive documentation of all waste disposal activities
- Plant Manager McNeil noted challenges in retrofitting existing lagoon systems to meet proposed standards
- Quality Control Manager Reynolds reported current monitoring procedures adequate for existing permit requirements

**Decisions Made:**
- Engage environmental consulting firm for comprehensive facility assessment
- Establish enhanced record-keeping protocols for all chemical usage and disposal
- Budget $75,000 for potential compliance upgrades in FY1978

#### 4. PERSONNEL AND SAFETY MATTERS

Plant Manager McNeil reported stable workforce with low turnover rates, though ongoing concerns regarding employee exposure to chromium compounds require continued attention. Recent safety training programs have improved awareness of proper handling procedures for hazardous materials.

**Safety Program Status:**
- Monthly safety meetings covering chemical handling protocols
- Personal protective equipment compliance at 95% during recent inspections
- No reportable injuries during Q1 1977 operations

**Discussion Points:**
- Employee health monitoring program under review by corporate medical staff
- Workers' compensation claims related to skin irritation from chromium exposure
- Training requirements for new employees handling hazardous materials

**Decisions Made:**
- Implement quarterly health screenings for employees with direct chemical exposure
- Enhance ventilation systems in tanning areas by September 1977
- Develop standardized training curriculum for hazardous materials handling

#### 5. COMMUNITY RELATIONS

Environmental Affairs Director Sullivan addressed recent community concerns regarding industrial operations in the Woburn area, noting increased public attention to environmental issues affecting residential neighborhoods. The discussion emphasized the importance of maintaining positive relationships with local officials and community groups.

**Community Engagement Activities:**
- Participation in Woburn Chamber of Commerce environmental committee
- Regular communication with municipal officials regarding facility operations
- Response protocols for citizen complaints and inquiries

**Discussion Points:**
- Recent media coverage of environmental issues in East Woburn area
- Coordination with other industrial facilities regarding community relations strategies
- Potential for organized citizen groups to influence regulatory enforcement

**Decisions Made:**
- Designate Patricia Reynolds as primary community liaison
- Establish quarterly meetings with Woburn city officials
- Develop informational materials explaining facility environmental practices

### ACTION ITEMS

1. **Plant Manager McNeil** - Schedule DEQE meeting for June 15, 1977, and prepare comprehensive facility documentation
2. **Environmental Affairs Director Sullivan** - Engage environmental consulting firm by June 30, 1977, for facility assessment
3. **Legal Counsel Mitchell** - Review all waste disposal contracts and permit requirements by July 15, 1977
4. **Quality Control Manager Reynolds** - Implement enhanced record-keeping protocols and community liaison responsibilities by June 1, 1977
5. **Production Supervisor Kowalski** - Coordinate equipment upgrades and ventilation improvements with corporate engineering staff

### NEXT MEETING

The next quarterly review meeting is scheduled for August 25, 1977, at 10:00 AM at the Riley Tannery facility. Corporate representatives will review progress on action items and assess any new regulatory developments affecting operations.

**Meeting Adjourned:** 1:15 PM
