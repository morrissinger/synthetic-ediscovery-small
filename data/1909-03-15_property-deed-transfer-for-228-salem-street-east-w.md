# WARRANTY DEED

**MIDDLESEX COUNTY REGISTRY OF DEEDS**  
**BOOK 3847, PAGE 421**

**Know all men by these presents,** that I, **HAROLD WHITMAN STEVENS** of Woburn, in the County of Middlesex and Commonwealth of Massachusetts, for and in consideration of the sum of **EIGHT THOUSAND AND NO/100 DOLLARS ($8,000.00)** paid by **JOHN JOSEPH RILEY** of Woburn, in the County of Middlesex and Commonwealth of Massachusetts, the receipt whereof is hereby acknowledged, do hereby give, grant, bargain, sell and convey unto the said **JOHN JOSEPH RILEY**, his heirs and assigns forever, a certain parcel of land with the buildings thereon, situated in the easterly part of said Woburn, bounded and described as follows:

## PROPERTY DESCRIPTION

**BEGINNING** at a stone bound set in the southerly side of Salem Street, said bound being distant two hundred and fifteen (215) feet westerly from the intersection of Salem Street with the Woburn Branch of the Boston and Maine Railroad right-of-way;

**THENCE** running southerly by land now or formerly of the Boston and Maine Railroad Corporation, three hundred and forty-two (342) feet to a stake and stones at the northerly bank of the Aberjona River;

**THENCE** running easterly by the northerly bank of said Aberjona River as it winds and turns, two hundred and sixty-eight (268) feet to a stake and stones;

**THENCE** running northerly by land now or formerly of the Woburn Leather Company, three hundred and eighteen (318) feet to a stone bound set in the southerly side of Salem Street;

**THENCE** running westerly by the southerly side of Salem Street, two hundred and fifty-three (253) feet to the point of beginning.

**CONTAINING** approximately two and one-quarter (2Â¼) acres of land, be the same more or less, and being the same premises conveyed to the said Harold Whitman Stevens by deed of Martha E. Whitman dated February 14, 1897, and recorded with Middlesex South District Registry of Deeds in Book 2641, Page 187.

## COVENANTS AND WARRANTIES

**TO HAVE AND TO HOLD** the above granted premises, with all the privileges and appurtenances thereto belonging, to the said **JOHN JOSEPH RILEY**, his heirs and assigns, to his and their use and behoof forever.

**AND I DO COVENANT** with the said grantee, his heirs and assigns, that I am lawfully seized in fee simple of the aforesaid premises; that they are free from all encumbrances except as hereinafter stated; that I have good right to sell and convey the same to the said grantee; and that I will, and my heirs, executors and administrators shall, **WARRANT AND DEFEND** the same to the said grantee, his heirs and assigns forever, against the lawful claims and demands of all persons.

## EXCEPTIONS AND RESERVATIONS

**EXCEPTING AND RESERVING** unto the Boston and Maine Railroad Corporation the right to maintain and operate its existing railroad tracks and appurtenant facilities along the westerly boundary of the herein described premises, together with reasonable rights of access thereto for maintenance and repair purposes.

**SUBJECT TO** existing easements for utilities and drainage as may appear of record or by visible evidence upon the premises, and subject to such restrictions and regulations as may be imposed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the ordinances of the City of Woburn regarding the operation of industrial facilities.

## INTENDED USE ACKNOWLEDGMENT

**IT IS UNDERSTOOD AND AGREED** that the said grantee intends to establish and operate upon the herein described premises a leather tanning and processing facility, and that such use shall be conducted in accordance with the customary practices of the leather manufacturing industry and in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the City of Woburn.

**THE GRANTEE HEREBY ACKNOWLEDGES** that the operation of industrial facilities carries with it certain responsibilities and duties of care toward neighboring properties and the general public, and that the grantee shall conduct all business operations in a manner consistent with the reasonable standards of industrial practice and with due regard for the health, safety and welfare of the surrounding community.

## EXECUTION AND ACKNOWLEDGMENT

**IN WITNESS WHEREOF,** I, the said **HAROLD WHITMAN STEVENS**, hereunto set my hand and seal this fifteenth day of March, in the year of our Lord one thousand nine hundred and nine.
