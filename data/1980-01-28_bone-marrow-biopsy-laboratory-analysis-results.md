## PATHOLOGIC INTERPRETATION

### Primary Diagnosis
**Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL), L1 morphology** according to French-American-British (FAB) classification system.

### Supporting Evidence
1. **Blast percentage:** 78% blasts exceeds the diagnostic threshold of 30% for acute leukemia
2. **Morphologic features:** Blast morphology consistent with lymphoblasts showing L1 characteristics
3. **Cytochemical pattern:** Negative myeloperoxidase reaction (performed on aspirate smears)
4. **Clinical correlation:** Age, presentation, and morphologic findings typical of pediatric ALL

### Secondary Findings
- **Severe pancytopenia** secondary to leukemic infiltration
- **Suppressed normal hematopoiesis** with marked reduction in erythropoiesis and megakaryopoiesis
- **Iron deficiency** likely secondary to chronic bleeding and poor nutritional status

---

## CLINICAL CORRELATION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

This bone marrow examination confirms the clinical suspicion of acute leukemia in this 7-year-old patient. The morphologic and cytochemical findings are diagnostic of acute lymphoblastic leukemia, L1 subtype, which represents the most common form of childhood leukemia.

### Recommended Additional Studies
1. **Immunophenotyping** to further classify ALL subtype (B-cell vs T-cell lineage)
2. **Cytogenetic analysis** for prognostic stratification
3. **Cerebrospinal fluid examination** to evaluate for CNS involvement
4. **Comprehensive metabolic panel** including LDH and uric acid levels

### Prognosis and Treatment Considerations
Pediatric ALL generally carries a favorable prognosis with current treatment protocols. However, the high blast percentage and clinical presentation suggest intermediate to high-risk disease requiring intensive chemotherapy protocols currently being developed through pediatric oncology cooperative groups.
