**Flash Point:** None (Non-flammable)

**Flammable Limits:** Non-flammable in air at normal temperatures  
**Density:** 12.2 lbs/gallon at 68°F

---

## SECTION IV - FIRE AND EXPLOSION DATA

**Flash Point:** None - Non-combustible  
**Flammable Limits:** Not applicable  
**Extinguishing Media:** Water spray, foam, dry chemical, carbon dioxide  
**Special Fire Fighting Procedures:** Use self-contained breathing apparatus. Cool containers with water spray.  
**Unusual Fire and Explosion Hazards:** At elevated temperatures above 1000°F, may decompose to form phosgene, hydrogen chloride, and chlorine gases.

---

## SECTION V - HEALTH HAZARD INFORMATION

**Threshold Limit Value:** 100 ppm (8-hour TWA) as established by ACGIH  
**Effects of Overexposure:**

**Acute Exposure:**
- Inhalation: Central nervous system depression, dizziness, headache, nausea
- Skin Contact: Defatting of skin, possible dermatitis
- Eye Contact: Mild irritation, tearing
- Ingestion: Nausea, vomiting, central nervous system effects

**Chronic Exposure:**
- Prolonged or repeated exposure may cause liver and kidney effects
- Some studies suggest possible carcinogenic potential in laboratory animals

**Emergency and First Aid Procedures:**
- **Inhalation:** Remove to fresh air. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen. Get medical attention.
- **Skin Contact:** Wash with soap and water. Remove contaminated clothing.
- **Eye Contact:** Flush with water for 15 minutes. Get medical attention if irritation persists.
- **Ingestion:** Do not induce vomiting. Give water or milk. Get medical attention immediately.

---

## SECTION VI - REACTIVITY DATA

**Stability:** Stable under normal conditions when properly stabilized  
**Incompatibility:** Strong alkalis, aluminum, magnesium, zinc powder  
**Hazardous Decomposition Products:** Phosgene, hydrogen chloride, chlorine  
**Hazardous Polymerization:** Will not occur  
**Conditions to Avoid:** High temperatures, contact with metals in presence of moisture

---

## SECTION VII - SPILL OR LEAK PROCEDURES

**Steps to be taken if material is released or spilled:**
1. Evacuate area of all unnecessary personnel
2. Provide adequate ventilation
3. Absorb liquid with sand, earth, or other non-combustible absorbent material
4. Place in containers for disposal according to local regulations
5. Flush area with water after removal of material
6. Do not allow material to enter sewers or waterways

**Waste Disposal Method:** Incinerate in approved facility capable of handling chlorinated compounds. Follow all federal, state, and local regulations for disposal.

---

## SECTION VIII - SPECIAL PROTECTION INFORMATION

**Respiratory Protection:** Use NIOSH-approved respirator with organic vapor cartridge when airborne concentrations exceed 100 ppm. Use supplied-air respirator for concentrations above 1000 ppm or in confined spaces.

**Ventilation:** Provide adequate general and local exhaust ventilation to maintain concentrations below TLV.

**Protective Gloves:** Neoprene, nitrile, or PVC gloves recommended for prolonged contact.

**Eye Protection:** Chemical safety goggles recommended when splash potential exists.

**Other Protective Equipment:** Impervious apron or clothing for extensive contact. Emergency eyewash and shower facilities should be available.

---

## SECTION IX - SPECIAL PRECAUTIONS

**Precautions to be taken in handling and storing:**
- Store in cool, dry, well-ventilated area away from heat sources
- Keep containers tightly closed when not in use
- Ground containers when transferring to prevent static electricity
- Do not store in aluminum, magnesium, or zinc containers
- Avoid prolonged or repeated skin contact

**Other Precautions:**
- Train personnel in proper handling procedures
- Maintain good housekeeping practices
- Monitor workplace air concentrations regularly
- Provide medical surveillance for workers with regular exposure
