# PROPERTY MANAGEMENT AGREEMENT

**BETWEEN**

**BEATRICE FOODS COMPANY**  
A corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 1526 North LaSalle Street, Chicago, Illinois 60610

**AND**

**JOHN J. RILEY COMPANY**  
A corporation organized and existing under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, with its principal place of business at 328 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801

---

**RECITALS**

WHEREAS, Beatrice Foods Company ("Beatrice") is the owner of certain real property located at 328 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801, more particularly described in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference (the "Property");

WHEREAS, the Property includes industrial facilities formerly operated as a tannery and currently utilized for various manufacturing and storage purposes;

WHEREAS, John J. Riley Company ("Riley") has operated business activities on portions of the Property since approximately 1968 under various informal arrangements with Beatrice;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to formalize their relationship regarding the use, management, and maintenance of the Property under the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I - GRANT OF MANAGEMENT RIGHTS

**1.1 Management Authority.** Beatrice hereby grants to Riley the exclusive right and authority to manage, operate, and maintain the Property for a term of five (5) years commencing on June 1, 1977, and terminating on May 31, 1982, unless sooner terminated in accordance with the provisions hereof.

**1.2 Scope of Management.** Riley's management authority shall include, but not be limited to:
- Day-to-day operational oversight of all facilities on the Property
- Coordination of maintenance and repair activities
- Management of waste disposal operations and procedures
- Supervision of chemical storage and handling protocols
- Liaison with regulatory agencies as may be required

**1.3 Permitted Uses.** The Property may be used for industrial manufacturing, chemical processing, waste treatment, and storage activities consistent with existing operations, provided such uses comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances.

## ARTICLE II - DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**2.1 Riley's Obligations.** Riley shall:

(a) Exercise reasonable care in the management and operation of the Property in accordance with industry standards applicable to industrial facilities of similar nature and complexity;

(b) Maintain the Property in good condition and repair, ordinary wear and tear excepted;

(c) Implement and maintain appropriate safety protocols for the handling, storage, and disposal of chemicals and industrial materials;

(d) Comply with all applicable environmental laws and regulations, including but not limited to the Federal Water Pollution Control Act, the Safe Drinking Water Act, and all regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Agency and the Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering;

(e) Maintain comprehensive records of all waste disposal activities, chemical usage, and environmental monitoring conducted on the Property;

(f) Provide Beatrice with quarterly reports detailing operational activities, maintenance performed, and any incidents or regulatory inquiries;

(g) Obtain and maintain all necessary permits, licenses, and approvals required for the operation of activities on the Property.

**2.2 Beatrice's Obligations.** Beatrice shall:

(a) Provide Riley with reasonable access to the Property and existing facilities;

(b) Cooperate with Riley in obtaining necessary permits and regulatory approvals;

(c) Reimburse Riley for approved capital improvements and extraordinary maintenance expenses exceeding $5,000 per occurrence;

(d) Maintain ownership responsibility for the Property and provide legal support for title-related matters.

## ARTICLE III - ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE

**3.1 Environmental Standards.** The parties acknowledge that industrial operations on the Property involve the use and disposal of various chemicals and industrial solvents. Riley agrees to conduct all operations in accordance with applicable environmental standards and to implement best practices for the protection of groundwater and surface water resources.

**3.2 Waste Disposal Protocols.** Riley shall establish and maintain written procedures for the proper disposal of industrial waste materials, including but not limited to:
- Segregation of different waste types
- Use of approved disposal contractors and facilities
- Maintenance of disposal records and manifests
- Regular inspection of waste storage areas

**3.3 Monitoring and Reporting.** Riley agrees to cooperate with any environmental monitoring or testing programs that may be implemented by regulatory agencies and to promptly report any spills, releases, or other environmental incidents to Beatrice and appropriate authorities.

## ARTICLE IV - INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

**4.1 Mutual Indemnification.** Each party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or resulting from:
- The negligent or wrongful acts or omissions of the indemnifying party
- Breach of any representation, warranty, or covenant contained herein
- Violation of applicable laws or regulations by the indemnifying party

**4.2 Environmental Liability.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, the parties acknowledge that environmental liability arising from historical operations on the Property may be subject to joint and several liability under applicable environmental laws. The parties agree to cooperate in good faith to address any such liability and to allocate responsibility based on the relative contribution of each party to any environmental contamination.

**4.3 Insurance Requirements.** Riley shall obtain and maintain comprehensive general liability insurance with minimum coverage limits of $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 in the aggregate, naming Beatrice as an additional insured.

## ARTICLE V - COMPENSATION

**5.1 Management Fee.** In consideration for Riley's management services, Beatrice shall pay Riley an annual management fee of $75,000, payable in quarterly installments of $18,750 each, due on the first day of each calendar quarter.

**5.2 Expense Reimbursement.** Beatrice shall reimburse Riley for all reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of its management duties, including utilities, routine maintenance, regulatory compliance costs, and insurance premiums, provided such expenses are supported by appropriate documentation.

## ARTICLE VI - TERMINATION

**6.1 Term.** This Agreement shall remain in effect for the full five-year term unless terminated earlier in accordance with the provisions hereof.

**6.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party:
- Materially breaches any provision hereof and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice
- Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy protection
- Violates any material environmental law or regulation

**6.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Riley shall surrender management of the Property to Beatrice and provide a complete accounting of all activities conducted during the term of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE VII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**7.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

**7.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

**7.3 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument executed by both parties.

**7.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is deemed invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**7.5 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
