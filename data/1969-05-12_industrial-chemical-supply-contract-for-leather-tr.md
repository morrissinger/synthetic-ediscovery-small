## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Supplier is engaged in the business of manufacturing, distributing, and supplying industrial chemical solvents and related products for commercial and industrial applications; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser operates a leather tanning and treatment facility requiring specialized chemical solvents for leather processing operations; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser desires to obtain a reliable supply of industrial-grade chemical solvents meeting specific technical requirements for leather treatment processes; and

WHEREAS, Supplier represents that it has the capacity, expertise, and resources to provide such chemical products in accordance with industry standards and applicable regulations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I - PRODUCTS AND SPECIFICATIONS

### 1.1 Product Categories
Supplier agrees to provide the following chemical products to Purchaser:

**A. Trichloroethylene (TCE)**
- Grade: Technical Grade, 99.5% minimum purity
- Packaging: 55-gallon steel drums
- Quantity: 24 drums per month minimum, 48 drums per month maximum
- Specification: ASTM D-5435 compliance

**B. Perchloroethylene (PCE)**
- Grade: Industrial Grade, 99.0% minimum purity  
- Packaging: 55-gallon steel drums
- Quantity: 12 drums per month minimum, 24 drums per month maximum
- Specification: Federal Specification O-T-620

**C. 1,1,1-Trichloroethane**
- Grade: Degreasing Grade, 98.5% minimum purity
- Packaging: 55-gallon steel drums
- Quantity: 18 drums per month minimum, 36 drums per month maximum
- Specification: Military Specification MIL-T-81533

### 1.2 Quality Assurance
All products shall be accompanied by certificates of analysis confirming compliance with specified purities and containing test results for moisture content, acidity, and residue levels. Supplier warrants that all products will meet or exceed stated specifications at time of delivery.

### 1.3 Additional Services
Supplier shall provide technical consultation regarding proper handling, storage, and application of supplied chemicals for leather treatment processes, including recommendations for worker safety procedures and facility ventilation requirements.

---

## ARTICLE II - DELIVERY AND PERFORMANCE

### 2.1 Delivery Schedule
Products shall be delivered to Purchaser's facility at 15 Olympic Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts, according to the following schedule:
- TCE: First and third Monday of each month
- PCE: Second Monday of each month  
- 1,1,1-Trichloroethane: Fourth Monday of each month

### 2.2 Delivery Requirements
All deliveries shall be made during normal business hours (7:00 AM to 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday) unless otherwise arranged. Supplier shall provide forty-eight (48) hours advance notice of all scheduled deliveries. Delivery vehicles must comply with Department of Transportation regulations for hazardous materials transport.

### 2.3 Title and Risk of Loss
Title to and risk of loss for all products shall pass to Purchaser upon delivery to Purchaser's designated receiving area. Supplier shall maintain appropriate insurance coverage for transportation risks until such transfer occurs.

---

## ARTICLE III - PRICING AND PAYMENT

### 3.1 Contract Pricing
Pricing for the contract term shall be as follows:
- Trichloroethylene: $0.47 per gallon
- Perchloroethylene: $0.52 per gallon
- 1,1,1-Trichloroethane: $0.41 per gallon

### 3.2 Price Adjustments
Prices may be adjusted annually based on changes in raw material costs, provided that Supplier gives Purchaser sixty (60) days written notice of any proposed price increase. No single annual increase shall exceed eight percent (8%) of the previous year's pricing.

### 3.3 Payment Terms
Payment shall be net thirty (30) days from invoice date. Purchaser may deduct a two percent (2%) discount for payments made within ten (10) days of invoice date. Late payments shall accrue interest at one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month.

---

## ARTICLE IV - TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Contract Term
This Agreement shall commence on June 1, 1969, and shall continue for a period of three (3) years, terminating on May 31, 1972, unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions hereof.

### 4.2 Renewal Option
Purchaser may extend this Agreement for an additional two-year period upon ninety (90) days written notice prior to expiration, subject to renegotiation of pricing terms.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision hereof and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice thereof.

---

### 5.1 Supplier Representations
Supplier represents and warrants that:
(a) It has full corporate power and authority to enter into this Agreement;
(b) All products supplied hereunder will conform to applicable federal and state regulations;
(c) It maintains all necessary licenses and permits for the manufacture and distribution of the products;

(b) It maintains appropriate facilities and equipment for the safe handling and storage of the products;
(c) It will use the products solely for legitimate industrial leather treatment purposes;
(d) It will comply with all applicable regulations regarding the use and disposal of chemical solvents.

### 6.1 Mutual Indemnification
Each party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party from and against any claims, damages, losses, or expenses arising from such party's negligent acts or omissions in the performance of this Agreement.

### 6.2 Product Liability
Supplier shall indemnify Purchaser against any claims arising from defects in the products supplied hereunder, provided that Purchaser uses such products in accordance with Supplier's written instructions and applicable safety guidelines.

Each party shall be responsible for its own compliance with applicable environmental laws and regulations. Purchaser acknowledges that it has sole responsibility for the proper use, handling, storage, and disposal of all products received hereunder in accordance with federal, state, and local environmental regulations.
