## PETITION FOR ADMINISTRATION OF THE ESTATE OF PATRICK TOOMEY, DECEASED

TO THE HONORABLE COURT:

The undersigned respectfully represents:

**1. PETITIONER INFORMATION**

That Richard Toomey of 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801, being of lawful age and sound mind, hereby petitions this Honorable Court for Letters of Administration upon the estate of Patrick Toomey, deceased.

**2. DECEDENT INFORMATION**

That Patrick Toomey, late of 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801, died on March 15, 1981, at Massachusetts General Hospital, Boston, Massachusetts, at the age of seven years and four months, having been born on November 12, 1973.

**3. DOMICILE AND RESIDENCE**

That said Patrick Toomey was at the time of his death domiciled in and an inhabitant of Woburn, Middlesex County, Massachusetts, and had been a resident of said address continuously from birth until death.

**4. CAUSE OF DEATH**

That the immediate cause of death was acute lymphocytic leukemia, as certified by Dr. Margaret Harrison, M.D., attending physician at Massachusetts General Hospital, on the Certificate of Death filed with the City Clerk of Boston on March 16, 1981.

**5. FAMILY RELATIONS**

That the said Patrick Toomey died leaving as his sole heirs-at-law and next of kin the following persons:

- **Father:** Richard Toomey, age 34, residing at 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801
- **Mother:** Patricia Toomey, age 32, residing at 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801
- **Sister:** Mary Catherine Toomey, age 9, residing at 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801
- **Brother:** Michael James Toomey, age 5, residing at 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801

**6. MARITAL STATUS**

That said decedent died unmarried and without issue.

**7. ESTATE ASSETS**

That to the best of petitioner's knowledge and belief, the estate of said decedent consists of the following assets:

- Savings account at Woburn Five Cents Savings Bank, Account No. 45789-3, approximate balance $125.00
- Personal effects and clothing of nominal value
- Potential claims for wrongful death and conscious pain and suffering arising from circumstances surrounding decedent's illness and death

**8. ESTATE LIABILITIES**

That the estate is subject to the following known debts and obligations:

- Massachusetts General Hospital, medical services rendered from January 1981 through March 1981, approximate amount $3,847.00
- Dr. Robert Chen, pediatric oncology services, approximate amount $1,250.00
- Children's Hospital Boston, prior treatment services 1979-1980, approximate amount $892.00
- Funeral expenses, McCarthy Funeral Home, Woburn, Massachusetts, amount $1,675.00

**9. NEED FOR ADMINISTRATION**

That administration of said estate is necessary for the following purposes:

a) To collect and preserve estate assets
b) To pay debts and expenses of administration
c) To investigate and pursue potential claims arising from the circumstances of decedent's death, including but not limited to claims against parties potentially responsible for environmental contamination that may have contributed to decedent's illness
d) To distribute any remaining assets to the lawful heirs

**10. PETITIONER'S INTEREST**

That your petitioner is the father of said decedent and has a direct interest in the proper administration of said estate as sole surviving parent and natural guardian of decedent's minor siblings.

**11. PRIORITY FOR APPOINTMENT**

That your petitioner is entitled to appointment as administrator, being the surviving parent of said minor decedent and having priority under Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 193, Section 1.

**12. BOND**

That your petitioner is willing to give bond for the faithful performance of his duties as administrator in such sum as this Court may require.

**13. CIRCUMSTANCES OF ILLNESS AND DEATH**

That the decedent Patrick Toomey was diagnosed with acute lymphocytic leukemia in September 1979 at the age of five years and ten months. That throughout his entire life, from prenatal development through his death, decedent resided at 47 Elm Street, Woburn, Massachusetts, and consumed water supplied by the Woburn municipal water system, including water from Wells G and H, which wells have recently been found to contain significant concentrations of industrial solvents including trichloroethylene and tetrachloroethylene.

That petitioner has reason to believe that decedent's illness and death may have been caused or contributed to by exposure to contaminated drinking water supplied by said municipal wells, and that such contamination may have resulted from improper disposal of industrial chemicals by various corporate entities operating in the vicinity of said wells.

**14. POTENTIAL LEGAL CLAIMS**

That petitioner believes the estate may have viable claims for wrongful death, conscious pain and suffering, and medical expenses against parties potentially responsible for contaminating the municipal water supply, and that administration is necessary to investigate and prosecute such claims within applicable statutes of limitations.

**WHEREFORE,** your petitioner respectfully requests that this Honorable Court:

1. Grant Letters of Administration upon the estate of Patrick Toomey, deceased, to Richard Toomey;

2. Authorize and direct the administrator to take possession of all estate assets;

3. Authorize the administrator to investigate and pursue all potential claims belonging to the estate;

4. Set such bond as the Court deems appropriate; and

5. Grant such other relief as may be just and proper.
