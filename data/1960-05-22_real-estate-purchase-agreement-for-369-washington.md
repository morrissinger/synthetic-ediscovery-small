# REAL ESTATE PURCHASE AGREEMENT

**PARTIES TO THIS AGREEMENT:**

**PURCHASER:** W.R. Grace & Co., a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Connecticut, with its principal place of business at 7 Hanover Square, New York, New York 10005

**SELLER:** Woburn Industrial Properties, Inc., a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, with its principal place of business at 125 Main Street, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801

---

## ARTICLE I - PROPERTY DESCRIPTION

WHEREAS, Seller is the owner in fee simple of certain real property located at 369 Washington Street East, Woburn, Middlesex County, Massachusetts, being more particularly described as follows:

A certain parcel of land with the buildings thereon, situated in East Woburn, Massachusetts, bounded and described as follows: Beginning at a point on the southerly side of Washington Street East, said point being approximately 200 feet easterly from the intersection of Washington Street East and Salem Street; thence running southerly parallel to Salem Street a distance of 450 feet; thence running easterly parallel to Washington Street East a distance of 300 feet; thence running northerly parallel to Salem Street a distance of 450 feet to the southerly side of Washington Street East; thence running westerly along the southerly side of Washington Street East a distance of 300 feet to the point of beginning.

Said premises containing approximately 3.1 acres, more or less, and being shown as Lot 15 on Plan Book 42, Page 18, Middlesex County Registry of Deeds.

The property is improved with a two-story brick industrial building containing approximately 18,000 square feet of floor space, together with associated loading docks, storage areas, and ancillary structures.

## ARTICLE II - PURCHASE PRICE AND TERMS

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements hereinafter set forth, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

**Purchase Price:** The total purchase price for the above-described property shall be Seventy-Five Thousand Dollars ($75,000.00), payable as follows:

1. **Deposit:** Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) paid upon execution of this Agreement, receipt of which is hereby acknowledged by Seller
2. **Balance:** Sixty-Five Thousand Dollars ($65,000.00) to be paid at closing by certified check or bank draft

**Closing Date:** This transaction shall close on or before June 30, 1960, at 10:00 A.M. at the offices of Middlesex Title Insurance Company, 45 School Street, Boston, Massachusetts.

## ARTICLE III - TITLE AND CONVEYANCE

Seller agrees to convey to Purchaser good and marketable title to the premises by warranty deed, free and clear of all liens, encumbrances, and defects, except for:

1. Zoning ordinances and building restrictions of record
2. Easements of record for utilities and municipal services
3. Real estate taxes for the year 1960, to be prorated as of the date of closing
4. Such other matters as may be disclosed by a current title examination

## ARTICLE IV - CONDITION OF PREMISES

**AS IS CONDITION:** Purchaser acknowledges that it has inspected the premises and agrees to accept the property in its present "AS IS" condition. Seller makes no representations or warranties concerning the condition of the buildings, improvements, soil, groundwater, or any environmental conditions affecting the property.

**INDUSTRIAL USE:** The parties acknowledge that the premises have been used for industrial purposes and that Purchaser intends to continue such use. Purchaser assumes full responsibility for compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local regulations governing industrial operations, including but not limited to regulations concerning waste disposal, air emissions, and water discharge.

**ENVIRONMENTAL MATTERS:** Purchaser acknowledges that industrial operations may have occurred on or adjacent to the premises and that such operations may have involved the use, storage, or disposal of chemicals, solvents, or other industrial materials. Purchaser waives any claim against Seller arising from environmental conditions existing as of the date of closing and assumes full responsibility for any remediation that may be required by governmental authorities.

## ARTICLE V - PURCHASER'S INTENDED USE

Purchaser represents that it intends to use the premises for the manufacture and processing of chemical products, including but not limited to industrial solvents, adhesives, and related chemical compounds. Purchaser acknowledges that such operations will require appropriate waste disposal methods and agrees to conduct all operations in accordance with applicable law and industry standards as they exist from time to time.

## ARTICLE VI - RISK OF LOSS

Risk of loss or damage to the premises by fire, casualty, or other cause shall remain with Seller until the date of closing. In the event of material damage to the premises prior to closing, Purchaser may, at its option, either:

1. Proceed with the closing and accept an assignment of any insurance proceeds, or
2. Terminate this Agreement and receive a full refund of the deposit

## ARTICLE VII - DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

**Purchaser Default:** If Purchaser fails to perform its obligations hereunder, Seller may retain the deposit as liquidated damages and pursue such other remedies as may be available at law or in equity.

**Seller Default:** If Seller fails to perform its obligations hereunder, Purchaser may either:

1. Seek specific performance of this Agreement, or
2. Terminate this Agreement and recover the deposit plus reasonable expenses incurred

## ARTICLE VIII - MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

**Binding Effect:** This Agreement shall be binding upon the heirs, successors, and assigns of the parties hereto.

**Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

**Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and may not be modified except by written instrument signed by both parties.

**Severability:** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

## ARTICLE IX - SPECIAL CONDITIONS

**Due Diligence Period:** Purchaser shall have until June 1, 1960, to complete its due diligence investigation of the property, including title examination, survey, and environmental assessment. Purchaser may terminate this Agreement without penalty if such investigation reveals conditions unsatisfactory to Purchaser in its sole discretion.

**Zoning Compliance:** Seller represents that the current use of the premises complies with applicable zoning ordinances. Purchaser's obligations hereunder are contingent upon the premises being properly zoned for Purchaser's intended industrial use.

**Utilities and Services:** Seller represents that the premises are served by municipal water, sewer, electricity, and gas services adequate for industrial operations.
