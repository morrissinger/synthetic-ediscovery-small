## WORK DESCRIPTION

**TASK:** Disposal of Empty Solvent Containers - Building 7 Storage Area

**LOCATION:** Building 7 Rear Storage Yard, adjacent to loading dock

**MATERIALS TO BE DISPOSED:**
- Approximately 35 empty 55-gallon steel drums (previously contained TCE - Trichloroethylene)
- 12 empty 30-gallon containers (previously contained PCE - Perchloroethylene) 
- 8 smaller containers, various sizes (mixed solvent residues)
- Associated metal scrap and contaminated materials

**DISPOSAL METHOD:** 
Transport containers to rear property area near drainage ditch for temporary storage. Containers may be punctured to ensure complete drainage of residual solvents before final disposal. Any remaining liquid contents should be allowed to drain into ground absorption area behind Building 7.

**SPECIAL INSTRUCTIONS:**
- Wear standard work gloves when handling containers
- Use forklift for transport of larger drums
- Ensure containers are emptied completely - residual solvents represent material cost
- Stack containers in designated area approximately 200 yards southeast of main building complex
- Area is accessible via service road behind Building 7

**ESTIMATED TIME:** 4-6 hours

**PERSONNEL ASSIGNED:**
- J. Sullivan (Lead)
- M. Kowalski 
- D. Peterson

---

## SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

Standard facility safety protocols apply. Containers previously held industrial cleaning solvents used in Cryovac manufacturing operations. While containers appear empty, use caution when handling due to potential residual vapors.

No special ventilation equipment required for this outdoor disposal operation. Work should be completed during daylight hours for visibility.

**ADDITIONAL REMARKS:**

This disposal operation is part of routine facility maintenance to clear storage areas for incoming material deliveries. The containers have been accumulating in Building 7 storage area following completion of recent production runs requiring increased solvent usage.

Current disposal method follows established facility procedures for empty container management. The designated disposal area has been used for similar operations in the past and provides adequate space for container storage pending final removal.

Weather conditions should be considered - avoid disposal operations during heavy rain to prevent unnecessary complications with ground conditions in disposal area.
