### WASTE DESCRIPTION AND CLASSIFICATION

#### **Shipment A - Organic Solvents**
- **Waste Code:** F001, F002, F005
- **Physical State:** Liquid
- **Container Type:** 55-gallon steel drums with tight-fitting lids
- **Number of Containers:** 24 drums
- **Total Weight:** 10,800 pounds (approximately)
- **Waste Description:** Mixed halogenated and non-halogenated solvents including trichloroethylene (TCE), tetrachloroethylene (PCE), methyl ethyl ketone, toluene, and xylene. Generated from degreasing operations and equipment cleaning in manufacturing processes.

#### **Shipment B - Chemical Residues**
- **Waste Code:** F006, D001
- **Physical State:** Semi-solid sludge
- **Container Type:** Fiber drums with polyethylene liners
- **Number of Containers:** 8 drums
- **Total Weight:** 2,400 pounds (approximately)
- **Waste Description:** Chemical treatment sludges containing heavy metals and organic compounds from wastewater treatment operations. pH testing indicates corrosive properties requiring special handling.

- **Waste Code:** P-Listed, U-Listed various

- **Container Type:** Various small containers packed in DOT-approved overpack drums
- **Number of Containers:** 6 overpack drums

### DISPOSAL METHOD AND JUSTIFICATION

The waste materials described above require disposal at a secure chemical landfill facility due to their hazardous characteristics and potential environmental impact if improperly managed. The selected disposal facility operates under EPA interim status and maintains appropriate containment systems to prevent groundwater contamination.

**Rationale for Off-Site Disposal:**
1. On-site treatment capabilities are insufficient for the volume and variety of waste generated
2. Secure landfill disposal provides long-term containment of hazardous constituents
3. Transportation to licensed facility ensures compliance with Massachusetts environmental regulations
4. Professional waste management reduces liability exposure for the company

---

### TRANSPORTATION AND HANDLING PROCEDURES

All containers have been inspected for structural integrity and proper labeling in accordance with DOT Hazardous Materials Regulations (49 CFR 100-199). Incompatible wastes are segregated to prevent dangerous reactions during transport. The selected transporter maintains appropriate insurance coverage and emergency response capabilities.

**Special Handling Requirements:**
- No smoking or open flames within 50 feet of vehicle
- Driver equipped with emergency response guide and spill cleanup materials
- Route planned to avoid residential areas and sensitive environmental locations
- Scheduled delivery during normal business hours for immediate processing
