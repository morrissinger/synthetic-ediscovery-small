## DISPOSAL OPERATIONS SUMMARY

**Excavation Details:**
- Primary pit: 12 feet x 20 feet x 8 feet deep
- Secondary pit: 8 feet x 15 feet x 6 feet deep
- Soil type: Clay and sand mixture
- Water table: Not encountered at burial depth

**Burial Procedure:**
1. Drums placed in excavated pits
2. Damaged drums double-wrapped in plastic sheeting
3. Lime applied over drum layer
4. Backfilled with excavated soil
5. Compacted with bulldozer tracks
6. Surface graded level with surrounding area

**Equipment Used:**
- Caterpillar D4 bulldozer (operator: T. Murphy)
- Backhoe attachment
- Hand tools for drum handling
- Plastic sheeting (200 sq ft used)
- Agricultural lime (4 bags applied)

---

## OBSERVATIONS AND NOTES

**Site Conditions:**
- Ground firm, good working conditions
- No standing water in excavation
- Wind direction: Southwest, minimal odor drift
- No residential properties in immediate vicinity
- Aberjona River approximately 300 yards northeast

**Operational Issues:**
- Three drums required field repairs due to leakage
- Strong chemical odors noted from several containers
- Some unlabeled drums accepted per client request
- Mixed waste compositions common in deliveries
- Burial depth adequate per company standards

**Personnel Safety:**
- Work gloves worn by all personnel
- Respirators used during strong odor periods
- Eye irritation reported by two workers
- No serious exposure incidents
- Standard company safety procedures followed

**Client Communications:**
- Grace facility representative observed operations
- Cryovac requested confirmation of deep burial
- Several clients emphasized quick disposal needs
- No special handling instructions beyond standard burial

## RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Continue monthly disposal operations at current site
2. Maintain burial depth minimum 6 feet
3. Consider additional lime application for odor control
4. Monitor for any groundwater seepage in excavations
