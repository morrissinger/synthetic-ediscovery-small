# CITY OF WOBURN
## DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC WORKS
### WORK ORDER AUTHORIZATION

**TO:** Municipal Contractors Inc.  
**FROM:** Robert F. Sullivan, Director of Public Works  
**DATE:** March 15, 1966  
**WORK ORDER NO:** WO-66-0315-001  
**PROJECT:** Municipal Well H Excavation and Drilling  

---

## PROJECT AUTHORIZATION

The City of Woburn hereby authorizes Municipal Contractors Inc. to commence excavation and drilling operations for the installation of **Municipal Well H** in accordance with the specifications outlined in Contract No. C-66-0201 dated February 8, 1966, and as approved by the Woburn Board of Public Works at their meeting of March 7, 1966.

## PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS

**Location:** East Woburn Industrial District, approximately 400 feet northeast of the Aberjona River, adjacent to property boundaries of industrial parcels on Olympia Avenue

**Drilling Depth:** 95 feet minimum depth, with capacity to extend to 110 feet pending geological conditions and water table assessment

**Well Casing:** 12-inch diameter steel casing with concrete pad foundation, pump housing structure to municipal standards

**Capacity Requirements:** Minimum pumping capacity of 400 gallons per minute, designed to supplement existing Well G operations during peak demand periods

**Timeline:** Work to commence no later than March 20, 1966, with completion target of May 15, 1966

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

This work order is issued in compliance with:
- Massachusetts Department of Public Health water supply regulations
- Woburn Municipal Code Chapter 15, Section 12 (Water Supply Infrastructure)
- State Environmental Health Guidelines for Municipal Wells (1965 revision)

The contractor shall obtain all necessary permits and shall coordinate with the Massachusetts Department of Public Health for required water quality testing upon completion of drilling operations.

## SITE CONDITIONS AND ACCESS

**Access Route:** Primary access via Olympia Avenue, secondary access from industrial service road adjacent to Aberjona River

**Utilities Clearance:** Underground utility clearance has been verified through Boston Edison Company and New England Telephone. Gas line clearance pending confirmation from Boston Gas Company.

**Environmental Considerations:** Site is located within the Aberjona River watershed. Contractor shall implement appropriate measures to prevent drilling fluid contamination of surface water during operations.

**Soil Conditions:** Preliminary geological survey indicates sandy loam topsoil (8-12 feet) over glacial till and bedrock formation. Groundwater table estimated at 15-20 feet depth.

## WORK SCOPE AND PROCEDURES

### Phase 1: Site Preparation (March 20-25, 1966)
- Clear and grade drilling site to 50' x 50' work area
- Install temporary access road suitable for drilling equipment
- Establish equipment staging area and material storage
- Install temporary fencing around work site perimeter

### Phase 2: Drilling Operations (March 28 - April 20, 1966)
- Mobilize drilling rig and commence boring operations
- Install temporary casing during drilling progression
- Collect geological samples at 10-foot intervals for city records
- Maintain drilling log with detailed depth and formation records
- Conduct preliminary water yield testing during drilling

### Phase 3: Well Completion (April 22 - May 10, 1966)
- Install permanent 12-inch steel well casing
- Construct concrete well pad and pump housing foundation
- Install sanitary well seal and protective casing
- Backfill and grade site to municipal specifications
- Conduct initial pump testing and water quality sampling

### Phase 4: Final Testing and Commissioning (May 12-15, 1966)
- Install temporary pumping equipment for yield testing
- Conduct 72-hour continuous pumping test
- Collect water samples for bacteriological and chemical analysis
- Submit samples to Massachusetts Department of Public Health laboratory
- Prepare final well completion report

## PAYMENT TERMS AND CONDITIONS

**Contract Value:** $18,500.00 as specified in Contract C-66-0201

**Payment Schedule:**
- 25% upon commencement of drilling operations
- 50% upon completion of well casing installation
- 25% upon final acceptance and water quality approval

**Performance Bond:** Contractor has posted performance bond No. PB-66-0154 with Merchants Insurance Company in the amount of $20,000.00

## SPECIAL CONDITIONS

**Water Quality Testing:** Upon completion of drilling, the contractor shall coordinate with the Massachusetts Department of Public Health for comprehensive water quality analysis including bacteriological testing, mineral content analysis, and chemical contamination screening. Well H shall not be placed into service until receiving written approval from state health authorities.

**Coordination with Well G Operations:** During construction, contractor shall coordinate with city water department to ensure no interruption to existing Well G operations located approximately 800 feet southwest of the Well H site.

**Industrial Area Considerations:** The work site is located within an active industrial district. Contractor shall coordinate with adjacent property owners including facilities operated by W.R. Grace Company, Cryovac Division, and other industrial tenants to minimize disruption to ongoing operations.

**Emergency Procedures:** In the event of equipment failure, groundwater contamination, or other emergency conditions, contractor shall immediately notify the Department of Public Works at MAyfair 4-7200 and cease operations until receiving further instruction.

## ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

Final acceptance of Well H installation shall be contingent upon:

1. Successful completion of 72-hour pumping test demonstrating minimum 400 GPM capacity
2. Water quality test results meeting or exceeding Massachusetts Department of Public Health standards for municipal water supply
3. Proper installation of all safety and sanitary features in accordance with state regulations
4. Final inspection approval by City of Woburn Department of Public Works

## AUTHORIZATION

This work order is hereby approved and authorized for execution in accordance with the terms and conditions specified herein.
