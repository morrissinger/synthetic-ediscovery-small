# CRYOVAC EQUIPMENT CLEANING AND SOLVENT DISPOSAL RECORD

**Facility:** Cryovac Inc. - Woburn Manufacturing Plant  
**Location:** 15 Olympia Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts 01801  
**Date:** November 29, 1971  
**Time:** 2:45 PM  
**Technician:** R. Morrison, Badge #247  
**Supervisor:** F. Kellerman  

---

## EQUIPMENT CLEANING OPERATIONS

### **Production Line A - Packaging Equipment**

**Equipment Cleaned:**
- Vacuum chamber units (4)
- Heat sealing stations (6)
- Conveyor belt systems
- Film feeding mechanisms

**Cleaning Solvents Used:**
- Trichloroethylene (TCE) - 45 gallons
- Perchloroethylene (PCE) - 32 gallons
- Methylene chloride - 18 gallons
- Industrial degreasing compound - 12 gallons

**Cleaning Procedure:**
Equipment shutdown completed at 1:30 PM. All vacuum chambers flushed with TCE solution using standard spray-down method. Heat sealing stations cleaned with PCE-soaked rags and brushes. Conveyor belts degreased using methylene chloride application. Film feeding mechanisms cleaned with industrial degreasing compound.

**Cleaning Duration:** 3 hours, 15 minutes  
**Personnel:** Morrison (lead), J. Santos, D. Mitchell

## MAINTENANCE NOTES

### **Equipment Condition Assessment**

**Vacuum Chamber Units:**
- Unit #1: Operating within normal parameters, minor seal wear noted
- Unit #2: Pressure readings slightly low, scheduled for seal replacement
- Unit #3: Normal operation, no issues observed  
- Unit #4: Temperature control requires calibration adjustment

**Heat Sealing Stations:**
All stations operating satisfactorily following cleaning. Temperature consistency improved after solvent cleaning of heating elements.

**Conveyor Systems:**
Belt tracking corrected during cleaning operation. Lubrication points serviced. No mechanical issues identified.

### **Solvent Effectiveness**

TCE proved most effective for vacuum chamber cleaning, removing all residual packaging material and adhesive buildup. PCE worked well on heat sealing station components. Methylene chloride effectively removed conveyor belt contamination. Industrial degreasing compound adequate for film feeding mechanism maintenance.
