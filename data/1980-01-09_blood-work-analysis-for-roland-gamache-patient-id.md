### CLINICAL INTERPRETATION

The laboratory findings reveal significant hematologic abnormalities consistent with **pancytopenia** (decreased white blood cells, red blood cells, and platelets). The pattern suggests possible bone marrow suppression or dysfunction.

**Key Findings:**
- **Leukopenia** with absolute neutropenia, increasing infection risk
- **Anemia** with normocytic, normochromic characteristics
- **Thrombocytopenia** with bleeding risk implications
- **Elevated liver enzymes** suggesting hepatic involvement
- **Coagulation abnormalities** with prolonged clotting times

**Differential Considerations:**
The constellation of findings, particularly in a pediatric patient from the East Woburn area, raises concern for:
1. Acute leukemia (requires bone marrow examination for definitive diagnosis)
2. Aplastic anemia
3. Myelodysplastic syndrome
4. Drug-induced bone marrow suppression
5. Viral infection with bone marrow involvement
6. Environmental toxin exposure
