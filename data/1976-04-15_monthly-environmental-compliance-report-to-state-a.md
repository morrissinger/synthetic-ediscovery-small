## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The John J. Riley Company hereby submits this monthly environmental compliance report for March 1976 operations at our Woburn manufacturing facility located at 185 New Boston Street, in accordance with Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 21, Section 43 and the conditions of our state-issued discharge permit. This report covers all industrial operations, waste management activities, and environmental monitoring conducted during the specified reporting period.

Our facility continues to operate within the parameters established by state environmental regulations, maintaining compliance with all applicable discharge limits and monitoring requirements. All required sampling and testing protocols have been conducted according to schedule, with results documented herein for departmental review.

## FACILITY OPERATIONS OVERVIEW

### Manufacturing Activities

During March 1976, the John J. Riley Company maintained normal production schedules across all operational divisions. Primary manufacturing activities included leather processing, chemical treatment operations, and finishing processes utilizing various industrial solvents and treatment chemicals. Total production volume for the month reached 847,320 square feet of processed leather goods, representing a 12% increase over February 1976 levels.

Our chemical usage during this reporting period included trichloroethylene (TCE) for degreasing operations, tetrachloroethylene (PCE) for dry cleaning processes, and various chromium-based compounds for tanning operations. All chemical storage and handling procedures followed established protocols, with inventory management conducted according to company safety standards.

### Waste Generation and Disposal

Total waste generation for March 1976 comprised 23,450 gallons of liquid industrial waste and 14,200 pounds of solid waste materials. Liquid waste streams included spent solvents, process wastewater, and cleaning solutions containing various chemical constituents necessary for leather processing operations.

Disposal of liquid industrial waste was conducted through our established ground disposal system, utilizing on-site infiltration areas designed to accommodate process discharge volumes. Solid waste materials were transported to approved disposal facilities by licensed hauling contractors in accordance with state requirements.

## OPERATIONAL INCIDENTS AND CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

### Incident Report - March 12, 1976

On March 12, 1976, facility personnel observed discoloration in groundwater monitoring well MW-2 during routine inspection. Investigation revealed temporary equipment malfunction in solvent recovery system, resulting in minor discharge of process chemicals to ground disposal area. Immediate corrective action included equipment repair and enhanced monitoring protocols.

The incident was contained within company property boundaries, and subsequent monitoring confirmed no off-site migration of contaminants. Additional sampling was conducted on March 15 and March 20 to verify system integrity, with all results indicating normal conditions.

### Corrective Measures Implemented

Following the March 12 incident, the company implemented enhanced inspection procedures for all chemical handling equipment, including daily visual inspections and weekly pressure testing of solvent storage tanks. Maintenance schedules were revised to ensure more frequent servicing of critical systems.

Staff training was conducted on March 25, 1976, covering proper response procedures for equipment malfunctions and spill containment protocols. All facility personnel completed required training modules and demonstrated competency in emergency response procedures.
