# DAILY CHEMICAL WASTE DISPOSAL LOG
## John J. Riley Company Tannery Operations
### 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts

**Date:** October 14, 1918  
**Shift:** Day Shift (6:00 AM - 6:00 PM)  
**Recording Officer:** Patrick Murphy, Floor Worker  
**Weather Conditions:** Clear, temperature 52Â°F, light wind from northwest  

---

## DISPOSAL SITE INFORMATION

**Primary Disposal Location:** Wooded area approximately 300 yards southwest of main tannery building  
**Secondary Location:** Natural depression near railroad embankment, west side of property  
**Access Route:** Dirt path from rear loading dock, past storage shed No. 3  

---

## MORNING OPERATIONS (6:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

### 6:15 AM - Initial Site Preparation
- Cleared debris from previous day's operations
- Noted standing water in disposal pit from weekend rainfall
- Water level approximately 18 inches deep, dark brown coloration observed
- Strong chemical odor present, consistent with tanning solution residuals

### 7:30 AM - First Disposal Run
**Load Contents:**
- 4 barrels spent chromium tanning solution (approximately 200 gallons total)
- 2 containers sulfuric acid waste from dehairing process (estimated 50 gallons)
- 1 barrel mixed organic solvents from leather finishing operations (45 gallons)

**Disposal Method:** Direct pour into excavated pit, contents mixed with existing standing water
**Personnel:** P. Murphy, T. O'Brien, J. Kowalski
**Notes:** Immediate reaction observed between fresh chemicals and standing water - bubbling and steam generation for approximately 10 minutes

### 9:45 AM - Second Disposal Run
**Load Contents:**
- 3 barrels lime solution waste (150 gallons)
- 1 container phenol-based disinfectant (25 gallons)
- Mixed hair and hide scraps soaked in processing chemicals (estimated 200 pounds)

**Disposal Method:** Solid materials dumped at pit edge, liquids poured directly into pit
**Notes:** Strong ammonia-like smell intensified after lime solution addition

### 11:20 AM - Third Disposal Run
**Load Contents:**
- 2 barrels spent sodium sulfide solution (100 gallons)
- 1 container mixed dye waste - reds and browns (30 gallons)
- Contaminated sawdust from floor cleanup (approximately 150 pounds)

**Disposal Method:** All materials deposited in pit, sawdust used to partially cover liquid surface
**Notes:** Pit now nearly at capacity, overflow beginning at southeast corner

---

## AFTERNOON OPERATIONS (12:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

### 1:15 PM - Site Assessment
- Pit overflow more pronounced after lunch break
- Dark liquid seepage observed flowing toward tree line
- Seepage path extends approximately 50 feet southeast toward natural drainage
- Vegetation along seepage path showing brown discoloration on leaves

### 2:30 PM - Fourth Disposal Run
**Load Contents:**
- 5 barrels mixed tanning waste (250 gallons)
- 1 container spent formic acid solution (40 gallons)
- 2 containers oil-based leather conditioning waste (60 gallons total)

**Disposal Method:** Due to pit overflow, materials dumped in secondary location near railroad embankment
**Notes:** Railroad embankment soil appears to absorb liquids rapidly, no immediate standing observed

### 4:00 PM - Fifth Disposal Run
**Load Contents:**
- 3 barrels chromium salt solution (150 gallons)
- 1 container mixed solvent waste from cleaning operations (35 gallons)
- Contaminated rags and leather scraps (approximately 100 pounds)

**Disposal Method:** Materials deposited at secondary site
**Notes:** Strong metallic taste in air, several workers complained of throat irritation

### 5:15 PM - Final Disposal and Site Securing
**Load Contents:**
- 2 barrels final rinse water from day's operations (100 gallons)
- 1 container mixed chemical residuals (20 gallons)
- General debris and contaminated materials (75 pounds)

**Disposal Method:** Deposited at secondary site, covered with available soil and vegetation
**Notes:** Both disposal sites secured for evening, tools cleaned and stored
