### DISPOSAL PROCEDURE

**14:20** - Initiated pumping operation from storage area to lagoon system. All three containers transported via forklift to discharge point at northeast corner of property.

**14:35** - Connected discharge hose to Container TCE-77-041. Verified lagoon water level at 3.2 feet below rim capacity. Current lagoon contents appear normal with slight chemical odor present.

**14:45** - Completed discharge of first container. No visible spillage. Material mixed readily with existing lagoon contents. Surface shows typical rainbow sheen from petroleum-based solvents.

**15:10** - Discharged Container TCE-77-042. Noted increased surface activity and stronger solvent odor. Lagoon level now at 2.8 feet below rim.

**15:25** - Final container TCE-77-043 discharged without incident. Total operation time: 65 minutes. Final lagoon level: 2.4 feet below rim capacity.

### ENVIRONMENTAL OBSERVATIONS

**Soil Conditions:** Ground around lagoon appears saturated. Some discoloration noted in soil approximately 15 feet east of lagoon perimeter, consistent with previous observations.

**Groundwater:** No visible seepage at surface level. Monthly monitoring well readings scheduled for next week per company protocol.

**Air Quality:** Strong solvent odor dissipated within 30 minutes of completion. No unusual vapor concentrations observed.

**Wildlife:** No dead fish or other animals observed in lagoon area. Vegetation around perimeter shows normal spring growth patterns.

### REGULATORY COMPLIANCE NOTES

This disposal operation conducted in accordance with current company waste management procedures as established in Company Manual Section 4.7.3. No specific federal or state permits required for on-site lagoon disposal of manufacturing waste solvents under current regulations.
