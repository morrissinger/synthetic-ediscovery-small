### CHIEF COMPLAINT

Patient brought in by mother with complaints of persistent fatigue, recurring nosebleeds, and unusual bruising over the past six weeks. Mother reports child has been "not himself" and appears pale and listless compared to normal activity levels.

### HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS

Mrs. Toomey reports that Patrick has experienced increasing fatigue since early July 1979. Initially attributed to summer heat and increased outdoor activity, but symptoms have progressively worsened. Patient complains of feeling "tired all the time" and has been reluctant to engage in usual childhood activities including bicycle riding and baseball with neighborhood children.

Nosebleeds began approximately four weeks ago, occurring 2-3 times per week, typically lasting 10-15 minutes. Episodes occur spontaneously, not associated with trauma or nose picking. Mother notes bleeding seems more difficult to control than typical childhood nosebleeds.

Bruising noticed on legs and arms over past three weeks. Mother describes bruises as appearing "out of nowhere" and notes they are larger and darker than would be expected from normal childhood bumps and falls. Several bruises noted on shins, forearms, and one prominent bruise on left shoulder without recalled trauma.

Patient also reports occasional headaches, described as "dull ache" primarily frontal, occurring 2-3 times per week. No associated nausea or visual changes.

Appetite has decreased noticeably over past month. Mother reports Patrick eating approximately half his normal portions and showing little interest in previously favored foods.

### PAST MEDICAL HISTORY

Birth history unremarkable. Full-term delivery, normal birth weight 7 lbs 2 oz. No significant illnesses during infancy or early childhood. Routine childhood immunizations up to date per Massachusetts Department of Public Health schedule. Previous medical visits limited to routine well-child examinations and minor injuries typical for active child.

No previous hospitalizations. No known allergies to medications or foods. No current medications.

### FAMILY HISTORY

Father Dennis Toomey, age 34, employed at Consolidated Edison, reports good health. Mother Margaret Toomey, age 32, housewife, reports good health. One sibling, sister Mary age 7, currently healthy. No family history of bleeding disorders, cancer, or significant genetic conditions. Maternal grandmother deceased age 68 from heart disease. Paternal grandparents living, both in good health.

### SOCIAL HISTORY

Patient lives with parents and younger sister in single-family home on Pine Street in East Woburn. Family has resided at current address since 1971. Patient attends Hurld Elementary School, currently entering 5th grade. Described as good student with no behavioral problems. Active child who enjoys outdoor activities, particularly baseball and bicycle riding in neighborhood.

Family drinks municipal water from East Woburn water system. No known exposure to chemicals or toxins. Father works outside Woburn area. No recent travel or unusual exposures reported.

### ASSESSMENT

10-year-old male presenting with 6-week history of fatigue, recurrent epistaxis, easy bruising, and hepatosplenomegaly on physical examination. Clinical presentation concerning for possible hematologic disorder.

Differential diagnosis includes:
1. Acute lymphoblastic leukemia - most concerning given constellation of symptoms
2. Idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura
3. Aplastic anemia
4. Viral syndrome with secondary thrombocytopenia

### PLAN

1. **Laboratory Studies:** Complete blood count with differential, peripheral blood smear, platelet count, PT/PTT, liver function tests. STAT processing requested.

2. **Referral:** Consultation with pediatric hematologist at Massachusetts General Hospital if laboratory results abnormal.

3. **Parent Education:** Discussed concerning nature of symptoms with mother. Advised to monitor for increased bleeding, fever, or worsening fatigue. Return immediately if symptoms worsen.

4. **Activity Restrictions:** Advised to avoid contact sports and activities with high risk of trauma pending laboratory results.

5. **Follow-up:** Telephone contact within 24 hours when laboratory results available. Return visit scheduled for August 17, 1979.

### ADDITIONAL NOTES

Mother questioned about other children in neighborhood with similar symptoms. Reports awareness of several other families with children experiencing health problems over past year, including the Anderson family on East Street and Kane family on Salem Street. Recommended that parents of affected children communicate with each other and consider discussing concerns with Woburn Board of Health.

Given recent community concerns about water quality in East Woburn area, advised family to consider using bottled water pending further evaluation of municipal water system.
