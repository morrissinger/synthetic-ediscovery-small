# CONSTRUCTION CONTRACT

**FOR THE ERECTION OF TANNERY FACILITY**

**SALEM STREET, EAST WOBURN, MASSACHUSETTS**

---

**THIS AGREEMENT** made this twenty-second day of June, in the year of our Lord one thousand nine hundred and nine, between **JOHN J. RILEY**, a citizen and resident of Woburn, Massachusetts, doing business as the John J. Riley Company, having his principal place of business at 15 Montvale Avenue, Woburn, Massachusetts (hereinafter referred to as "Owner"), and **THOMAS MCKENNA CONSTRUCTION COMPANY**, a partnership organized under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, having its principal place of business at 47 Washington Street, Boston, Massachusetts (hereinafter referred to as "Contractor").

## WITNESSETH:

**WHEREAS**, Owner desires to construct a tannery facility for the processing of animal hides and leather goods manufacturing upon certain premises located at 228 Salem Street in the East Woburn section of Woburn, Massachusetts, being more particularly described as Lot 15 on Plan Book 23, Page 87, recorded in the Middlesex County Registry of Deeds; and

**WHEREAS**, Contractor represents that it possesses the necessary skill, experience, and financial capacity to construct said facility in accordance with the specifications and requirements hereinafter set forth; and

**WHEREAS**, the parties desire to set forth the terms and conditions governing the construction of said tannery facility;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements hereinafter contained, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I - SCOPE OF WORK

The Contractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, and services necessary for the complete construction of a tannery facility upon the premises described above, in accordance with the plans and specifications prepared by Architect William H. Prescott, dated May 15, 1909, which plans and specifications are incorporated herein by reference and made a part of this contract.

The work shall include, but not be limited to:

1. **Main Processing Building**: A two-story brick and timber frame structure measuring approximately 120 feet by 80 feet, with reinforced concrete foundations extending to a depth of not less than eight feet below grade level.

2. **Chemical Storage Facility**: A single-story brick structure measuring 40 feet by 30 feet, designed for the storage of tanning chemicals including chromium sulfate, sodium sulfide, lime, and other materials required for leather processing operations.

3. **Waste Treatment Basin**: Excavation and construction of a settling basin measuring 60 feet by 40 feet by 12 feet deep, lined with clay and designed to receive liquid waste from tanning operations prior to discharge.

4. **Water Supply Infrastructure**: Installation of a private well system capable of supplying not less than 5,000 gallons per day for processing operations, including pump house and distribution piping.

5. **Railroad Siding**: Construction of approximately 200 feet of railroad siding connecting to the existing Boston & Maine Railroad tracks to facilitate receipt of raw materials and shipment of finished goods.

## ARTICLE II - MATERIALS AND WORKMANSHIP

All materials furnished shall be new, of good quality, and in accordance with the specifications. All work shall be performed in a good and workmanlike manner in accordance with the best practices of the respective trades and in compliance with all applicable building codes and ordinances of the City of Woburn and the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

The Contractor warrants that all work performed and materials furnished shall be free from defects for a period of one year from the date of completion and acceptance by Owner.

## ARTICLE III - CONTRACT PRICE AND PAYMENT TERMS

The total contract price for the work described herein shall be **EIGHT THOUSAND FIVE HUNDRED DOLLARS ($8,500.00)**, payable as follows:

1. Upon execution of this contract: $1,000.00
2. Upon completion of foundation work: $2,000.00  
3. Upon completion of structural framing: $2,500.00
4. Upon completion of roofing and exterior walls: $2,000.00
5. Upon final completion and acceptance: $1,000.00

## ARTICLE IV - TIME OF PERFORMANCE

Work shall commence within ten (10) days of the execution of this contract and shall be substantially completed within one hundred twenty (120) days thereafter, weather permitting. Time is of the essence in this contract.

The Contractor acknowledges that Owner intends to commence tannery operations by October 1, 1909, and agrees to use best efforts to complete the work in time to meet this objective.

## ARTICLE V - ENVIRONMENTAL AND SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

The Contractor shall exercise due care in the construction of the waste treatment basin to ensure proper containment of processing wastes. The basin shall be constructed with adequate capacity to handle the anticipated volume of liquid waste from tanning operations, estimated at approximately 2,000 gallons per day.

The Contractor shall locate the waste treatment basin at a sufficient distance from the Aberjona River to prevent direct discharge of untreated waste into said waterway, in accordance with customary practices of the leather manufacturing industry.

All excavation work shall be performed with due regard for the protection of groundwater resources, and any unexpected water infiltration encountered during construction shall be promptly reported to Owner.

## ARTICLE VI - PERMITS AND APPROVALS

Owner shall be responsible for obtaining all necessary permits and approvals from municipal and state authorities. Contractor shall cooperate with Owner in providing such information and documentation as may be required for permit applications.

## ARTICLE VII - INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

Contractor shall maintain adequate insurance coverage for the protection of workers and the public during construction operations. Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Owner from any claims arising out of Contractor's performance of the work hereunder.

## ARTICLE VIII - CHANGES AND MODIFICATIONS

No changes or modifications to this contract shall be valid unless agreed to in writing and signed by both parties.

## ARTICLE IX - DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

In the event of default by either party, the non-defaulting party shall have all remedies available at law or in equity, including the right to terminate this contract upon written notice.

## ARTICLE X - GOVERNING LAW

This contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
