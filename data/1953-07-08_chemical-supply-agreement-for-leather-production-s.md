# CHEMICAL SUPPLY AGREEMENT

**AGREEMENT** made this 8th day of July, 1953, between **MASSACHUSETTS CHEMICAL SUPPLY COMPANY**, a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, having its principal place of business at 1247 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, Massachusetts (hereinafter referred to as "Supplier"), and **JOHN J. RILEY COMPANY**, a Massachusetts corporation engaged in leather tanning operations, having its principal place of business at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts (hereinafter referred to as "Purchaser").

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Supplier is engaged in the business of manufacturing, distributing, and selling industrial chemicals and solvents for commercial and industrial applications; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser operates a leather tanning facility requiring specialized chemical solvents for leather processing, degreasing, and finishing operations; and

WHEREAS, Purchaser desires to obtain a reliable supply of industrial solvents and chemicals necessary for its leather production operations; and

WHEREAS, Supplier is willing to provide such chemicals and solvents under the terms and conditions hereinafter set forth;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. PRODUCTS AND SPECIFICATIONS

Supplier agrees to manufacture and deliver to Purchaser the following chemical products (the "Products"):

**a) Trichloroethylene (TCE)** - Industrial grade, minimum 99.5% purity, in 55-gallon steel drums, for use in leather degreasing operations. Estimated monthly requirement: 200-300 gallons.

**b) Perchloroethylene (PCE)** - Dry cleaning grade, minimum 99.0% purity, in 55-gallon steel drums, for use in leather finishing and spot cleaning. Estimated monthly requirement: 150-250 gallons.

**c) Carbon Tetrachloride** - Technical grade, minimum 98.0% purity, in 30-gallon steel containers, for specialized cleaning applications. Estimated monthly requirement: 50-100 gallons.

**d) Mixed Hydrocarbon Solvents** - Proprietary blend for leather conditioning, in 30-gallon containers. Estimated monthly requirement: 75-125 gallons.

All Products shall conform to industry standards for leather processing applications and shall be accompanied by certificates of analysis upon delivery. Supplier warrants that all Products will be of merchantable quality and suitable for Purchaser's intended leather manufacturing purposes.

## 2. DELIVERY AND ACCEPTANCE

Supplier shall deliver Products to Purchaser's facility at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts, or to such other location as Purchaser may designate in writing. Deliveries shall be made by truck transport in suitable containers as specified above. Purchaser shall provide reasonable access to its property for delivery vehicles and shall designate appropriate storage areas for chemical containers.

Delivery schedule shall be as follows: Initial delivery within ten (10) days of execution of this Agreement, with subsequent deliveries to be made monthly or as requested by Purchaser with forty-eight (48) hours advance notice. Time shall not be of the essence for deliveries unless specifically agreed to in writing.

Risk of loss shall pass to Purchaser upon delivery to the designated location. Purchaser shall inspect all shipments upon receipt and shall notify Supplier in writing of any shortages, damages, or nonconforming Products within five (5) business days of delivery.

## 3. PRICING AND PAYMENT TERMS

The prices for Products shall be as follows, effective as of the date of this Agreement:

- Trichloroethylene: $0.42 per gallon, F.O.B. destination
- Perchloroethylene: $0.38 per gallon, F.O.B. destination  
- Carbon Tetrachloride: $0.51 per gallon, F.O.B. destination
- Mixed Hydrocarbon Solvents: $0.29 per gallon, F.O.B. destination

Prices are subject to adjustment upon sixty (60) days written notice from Supplier due to changes in raw material costs, transportation expenses, or applicable taxes. Payment terms shall be net thirty (30) days from date of invoice. A service charge of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month may be applied to overdue accounts.

## 4. TERM AND RENEWAL

This Agreement shall commence on July 8, 1953, and shall continue for a period of three (3) years, terminating on July 7, 1956, unless sooner terminated in accordance with the provisions hereof. This Agreement may be renewed for additional one-year terms by mutual written agreement of the parties.

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon ninety (90) days written notice to the other party. In the event of material breach by either party, the non-breaching party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice, provided that the breaching party shall have ten (10) days to cure any such breach after receiving written notice thereof.

## 5. HANDLING AND STORAGE REQUIREMENTS

Purchaser acknowledges that the Products supplied hereunder are industrial chemicals requiring proper handling, storage, and disposal procedures. Purchaser agrees to:

**a)** Store all Products in appropriate containers in well-ventilated areas away from heat sources, open flames, and incompatible materials;

**b)** Provide adequate training to employees handling said Products regarding safe handling procedures and potential health hazards;

**c)** Maintain appropriate safety equipment including ventilation systems, protective clothing, and emergency response materials;

**d)** Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local regulations regarding the storage, use, and handling of industrial chemicals and solvents.

Supplier shall provide Material Safety Data information and handling recommendations for all Products delivered hereunder. Purchaser assumes full responsibility for the safe storage, handling, use, and disposal of all Products after delivery and acceptance.

## 6. WASTE DISPOSAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE

Purchaser shall be solely responsible for the proper disposal of all waste materials, spent solvents, container residues, and byproducts resulting from the use of Products supplied hereunder. Purchaser agrees to dispose of all such waste materials in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local environmental regulations and industry standards.

Purchaser may dispose of waste solvents and chemical residues through approved industrial waste disposal methods, including but not limited to: licensed commercial waste disposal services, approved incineration facilities, or other methods deemed acceptable by regulatory authorities. On-site disposal methods, including but not limited to burial, surface discharge, or lagoon storage, shall be conducted in accordance with applicable regulations and with due regard for environmental protection.

Supplier makes no representations or warranties regarding disposal methods and expressly disclaims any responsibility for Purchaser's waste disposal practices or any environmental consequences resulting therefrom.

## 7. INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

Purchaser agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Supplier from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or resulting from:

**a)** Purchaser's use, handling, storage, or disposal of Products supplied hereunder;

**b)** Any breach of this Agreement by Purchaser;

**c)** Any negligent or wrongful acts or omissions of Purchaser, its employees, agents, or contractors in connection with the Products.

Supplier's liability for any breach of warranty or other claim relating to the Products shall be limited to replacement of nonconforming Products or refund of the purchase price, at Supplier's option. IN NO EVENT SHALL SUPPLIER BE LIABLE FOR CONSEQUENTIAL, INCIDENTAL, OR SPECIAL DAMAGES.

## 8. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable for any delay or failure in performance under this Agreement due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, fire, flood, earthquake, war, civil disturbance, labor disputes, government regulations, or shortage of materials or transportation facilities.

## 9. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTES

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. Any disputes arising hereunder shall be resolved in the courts of competent jurisdiction in Middlesex County, Massachusetts.

## 10. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.
