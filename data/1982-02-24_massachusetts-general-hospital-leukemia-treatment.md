## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This compilation provides comprehensive medical treatment records for pediatric patients from the Woburn, Massachusetts area who received leukemia treatment at Massachusetts General Hospital between 1970-1982. These records have been prepared in response to your legal request dated February 10, 1982, concerning potential environmental exposure-related childhood leukemia cases.

The following compilation includes detailed treatment histories, diagnostic findings, therapeutic interventions, and clinical outcomes for seven (7) pediatric patients, all residents of the East Woburn area served by municipal Wells G and H.

## PATIENT RECORD COMPILATION

### PATIENT A: JAMES ANDERSON
**DOB:** July 16, 1968  
**Address:** 11 Orange Street, Woburn, MA  
**Parent/Guardian:** Anne Anderson  
**Date of Death:** January 18, 1981

**DIAGNOSTIC HISTORY:**
- **Initial Presentation:** January 15, 1972 (Age 3 years, 6 months)
- **Presenting Symptoms:** Persistent fever (101-103°F), lethargy, bruising on extremities, pallor
- **Initial CBC:** WBC 47,000/mm³ (normal 4,000-10,000), Hemoglobin 6.2 g/dL (normal 11-14), Platelets 18,000/mm³ (normal 150,000-400,000)
- **Bone Marrow Biopsy (1/18/72):** 89% lymphoblasts, confirming acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL)

**TREATMENT PROTOCOL:**
- **Phase I Induction (1972):** Vincristine, Prednisone, L-Asparaginase
- **CNS Prophylaxis:** Intrathecal Methotrexate, cranial radiation (1,800 rads)
- **Maintenance Therapy (1972-1979):** Mercaptopurine, Methotrexate, periodic Vincristine/Prednisone pulses

**CLINICAL COURSE:**
James achieved initial remission within 6 weeks of treatment initiation. However, his clinical course was marked by multiple complications including:
- Severe mucositis requiring hospitalization (March 1973, June 1974, September 1975)
- Pneumocystis carinii pneumonia (August 1976)
- Avascular necrosis of bilateral femoral heads (December 1977)
- Chronic steroid-induced growth retardation

**RELAPSE AND TERMINAL PHASE:**
- **First Relapse:** September 1979 - CNS involvement detected via lumbar puncture
- **Salvage Therapy:** High-dose Cytarabine, Daunorubicin - partial response
- **Second Relapse:** March 1980 - Bone marrow and testicular involvement
- **Terminal Phase:** Progressive disease despite intensive chemotherapy
- **Final Hospitalization:** December 15, 1980 - January 18, 1981

**DOCUMENTED SUFFERING:**
Medical records document extensive conscious pain and suffering throughout treatment:
- Chronic neuropathic pain from vincristine toxicity
- Severe oral mucositis causing inability to eat or speak for weeks
- Bone pain from leukemic infiltration and steroid-induced necrosis
- Psychological distress documented by social work consultations
- **Pain Assessment Scores (1980):** Consistently 8-10/10 on pediatric pain scale

### PATIENT B: KEVIN KANE JR.
**DOB:** March 22, 1967  
**Address:** 429 East Street, Woburn, MA  
**Parent/Guardian:** Kevin Kane Sr., Donna Kane  

**DIAGNOSTIC HISTORY:**
- **Initial Presentation:** August 10, 1973 (Age 6 years, 5 months)
- **Presenting Symptoms:** Recurrent infections, easy bruising, abdominal distension
- **Diagnosis:** Acute lymphocytic leukemia with hepatosplenomegaly
- **Bone Marrow:** 78% lymphoblasts

**TREATMENT AND OUTCOMES:**
- Initial remission achieved October 1973
- Maintenance therapy continued through 1976
- **Relapse:** January 1977 - meningeal leukemia
- **Death:** April 3, 1977, following CNS progression

**PAIN DOCUMENTATION:**
Extensive documentation of headaches, nausea, and neurological symptoms during terminal phase. Parents reported child's statements of severe head pain and visual disturbances.
