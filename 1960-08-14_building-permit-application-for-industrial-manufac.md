## PROJECT DESCRIPTION

**Proposed Use:** Chemical Manufacturing and Processing Facility  
**Project Title:** Cryovac Division Manufacturing Plant - Phase II Expansion  

**WHEREAS,** W.R. Grace & Company hereby submits this application for a building permit to construct additional manufacturing facilities at the above-referenced property located on Washington Street in the City of Woburn, Massachusetts; and

**WHEREAS,** the proposed construction consists of a 24,000 square foot industrial building designed for the manufacture of specialized plastic films and packaging materials under the Cryovac Division operations; and

**WHEREAS,** the applicant has conducted due diligence regarding compliance with all applicable building codes, zoning ordinances, and industrial safety regulations as established by the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the City of Woburn;

**NOW THEREFORE,** W.R. Grace & Company respectfully requests approval of this building permit application under the following specifications:

## CONSTRUCTION SPECIFICATIONS

### Building Dimensions and Structure
- **Total Floor Area:** 24,000 square feet
- **Building Height:** 32 feet maximum
- **Construction Type:** Steel frame with concrete block walls
- **Roof System:** Built-up asphalt and gravel roofing
- **Foundation:** Reinforced concrete slab on grade with perimeter footings extending 4 feet below grade

### Industrial Equipment and Utilities
- **Electrical Service:** 480-volt, 3-phase service with 800-amp main disconnect
- **Water Supply:** Connection to municipal water system via 6-inch main on Washington Street
- **Sewer Connection:** Industrial discharge line connecting to municipal sewer system
- **Heating System:** Steam boiler system with overhead distribution
- **Ventilation:** Industrial exhaust system with stack height of 45 feet

### Manufacturing Operations
The proposed facility will house equipment for the production of polyethylene and polyvinylidene chloride films used in food packaging applications. Manufacturing processes will include:

- Extrusion operations for plastic film production
- Coating applications using various chemical compounds including adhesives and barrier coatings
- Printing and converting operations for finished packaging products
- Quality control laboratory facilities
- Raw material storage areas for polymer resins and chemical additives

### Chemical Storage and Handling
The facility will incorporate dedicated storage areas for manufacturing chemicals including but not limited to:
- Trichloroethylene (TCE) for equipment cleaning and degreasing operations
- Perchloroethylene (PCE) for specialized cleaning processes
- Various organic solvents required for coating and adhesive applications
- Polymer resins and plastic compounds

All chemical storage areas will be constructed with appropriate containment systems and ventilation as required by industrial safety standards.

## SITE DEVELOPMENT

### Property Improvements
- **Parking Area:** Asphalt parking lot accommodating 75 vehicles
- **Loading Facilities:** Three truck loading bays with concrete aprons
- **Landscaping:** Minimal landscaping consisting of grass areas and foundation plantings
- **Drainage:** Surface water drainage directed to existing storm sewer system

### Environmental Considerations
The applicant acknowledges that manufacturing operations will generate industrial wastewater requiring treatment prior to discharge into the municipal sewer system. A wastewater treatment system will be installed to ensure compliance with discharge regulations established by the Massachusetts Water Pollution Control Board.

Waste disposal practices will conform to standard industrial protocols, with solid wastes transported to approved disposal facilities and liquid wastes managed through appropriate treatment and discharge procedures.

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### Building Code Compliance
This application has been prepared in accordance with the Massachusetts State Building Code and local building ordinances. The proposed construction meets all requirements for industrial occupancy including:
- Fire safety systems and emergency exits
- Structural load calculations for industrial equipment
- Industrial ventilation requirements
- Electrical code compliance for manufacturing operations

### Zoning Compliance
The proposed use is permitted within the IM-2 Industrial Manufacturing zoning district. The building placement and site development comply with required setbacks and coverage limitations as specified in the Woburn Zoning Ordinance.

### Health and Safety Standards
Manufacturing operations will comply with industrial safety regulations established by the Massachusetts Department of Labor and Industries. Employee safety equipment and procedures will meet or exceed industry standards for chemical manufacturing operations.

## CONSTRUCTION TIMELINE

**Estimated Construction Start:** September 15, 1960  
**Estimated Completion:** March 30, 1961

Construction will proceed in phases to minimize disruption to existing operations at the Washington Street facility. The general contractor, Beacon Construction Company of Boston, Massachusetts, has been selected to oversee all construction activities.
