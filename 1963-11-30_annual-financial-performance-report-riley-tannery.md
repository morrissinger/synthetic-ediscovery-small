# ANNUAL FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE REPORT
## RILEY TANNERY OPERATIONS 1963

**TO:** Beatrice Foods Corporation - Corporate Finance Division  
**FROM:** J.M. Henderson, Chief Accountant - Riley Tannery Division  
**DATE:** November 30, 1963  
**RE:** Annual Financial Assessment and Operational Summary

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Riley Tannery operations at 228 Salem Street, East Woburn, Massachusetts, completed fiscal year 1963 with gross revenues of $847,250, representing a 12.3% increase over the previous year's performance of $754,680. Net operating income reached $127,088, yielding a profit margin of 15.0% on total sales volume. The facility processed approximately 485,000 square feet of finished leather products during the reporting period, maintaining our position as a reliable supplier within Beatrice Foods' diversified manufacturing portfolio.

Operational efficiency improvements implemented throughout 1963 contributed significantly to enhanced profitability, particularly in the areas of chemical processing optimization and waste management protocols. The tannery's strategic location continues to provide competitive advantages in transportation costs and raw material procurement, while our established customer relationships in the New England leather goods market ensure consistent demand for our premium-grade leather products.

## REVENUE ANALYSIS

### Primary Revenue Streams

**Finished Leather Sales:** $692,400 (81.7% of total revenue)
- Premium cowhide leather: $428,650
- Standard grade leather products: $186,320
- Specialty leather processing: $77,430

**Contract Processing Services:** $154,850 (18.3% of total revenue)
- Third-party hide processing: $98,240
- Custom finishing services: $56,610

The leather market demonstrated robust demand throughout 1963, with particular strength in the automotive upholstery sector and traditional leather goods manufacturing. Our premium cowhide products commanded prices averaging $1.42 per square foot, representing a 8.5% increase over 1962 pricing levels. The expansion of contract processing services proved particularly profitable, generating higher margins than direct leather sales while utilizing existing facility capacity more efficiently.

## OPERATIONAL EXPENSES

### Direct Manufacturing Costs: $485,670

**Raw Materials and Chemicals:** $267,340
- Raw hides and skins: $189,850
- Tanning chemicals (chromium salts, acids): $45,290
- Processing solvents and degreasers: $32,200

**Labor Costs:** $142,680
- Production workers (18 full-time employees): $98,450
- Supervisory staff: $28,930
- Benefits and payroll taxes: $15,300

**Utilities and Facility Operations:** $75,650
- Electricity and heating fuel: $31,240
- Water and sewer services: $18,410
- Equipment maintenance and repairs: $26,000

### Administrative and General Expenses: $234,492

The facility's chemical processing requirements remained consistent with previous years, utilizing established tanning methodologies that have proven both cost-effective and reliable. Our chemical inventory management system ensures adequate supplies of chromium-based tanning agents, degreasing solvents, and processing acids while minimizing storage costs and waste disposal expenses.

## CAPITAL IMPROVEMENTS AND EQUIPMENT

During 1963, the Riley Tannery invested $43,850 in facility improvements and equipment upgrades:

- **Chemical Storage Expansion:** $18,200 for additional storage capacity for tanning chemicals and processing solvents
- **Waste Management Infrastructure:** $12,650 for improved waste handling systems and disposal protocols
- **Production Equipment Maintenance:** $8,750 for major overhauls of tanning drums and finishing equipment
- **Facility Improvements:** $4,250 for building maintenance and safety upgrades

The expanded chemical storage capacity addresses our growing inventory requirements while ensuring compliance with local storage regulations. The enhanced waste management systems improve operational efficiency and support our commitment to responsible disposal of manufacturing byproducts.

## ENVIRONMENTAL AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

The Riley Tannery maintains full compliance with all applicable municipal and state regulations governing industrial operations. Our waste disposal procedures follow established protocols for the handling of tanning chemicals, processing solvents, and manufacturing residues. The facility's location provides convenient access to approved disposal methods, including on-site waste management areas that meet current regulatory standards.

Chemical usage during 1963 included approximately 12,400 gallons of various processing solvents, 8,750 pounds of chromium-based tanning agents, and 5,200 gallons of degreasing compounds. All chemical handling and disposal procedures conform to industry best practices and regulatory requirements established by local health authorities.

## MARKET OUTLOOK AND STRATEGIC POSITIONING

The New England leather industry continues to demonstrate stability, with consistent demand from traditional manufacturing sectors including footwear, automotive, and leather goods production. The Riley Tannery's established reputation for quality products and reliable delivery schedules positions the operation favorably for continued growth within the Beatrice Foods manufacturing portfolio.

Regional competition remains manageable, with our facility's strategic location and operational efficiency providing sustainable competitive advantages. The proximity to transportation infrastructure and established supplier relationships support our ability to maintain cost-effective operations while serving both local and regional markets.

## RECOMMENDATIONS FOR 1964

Based on current market conditions and operational performance, we recommend the following strategic initiatives for the upcoming fiscal year:

1. **Production Capacity Expansion:** Investigate opportunities to increase processing capacity by 15-20% to meet growing demand for premium leather products.

2. **Chemical Processing Optimization:** Continue refinement of tanning and finishing processes to improve efficiency and reduce material costs.

3. **Market Development:** Explore expansion into specialized leather products for emerging industrial applications.

4. **Facility Modernization:** Evaluate additional capital improvements to enhance operational efficiency and maintain competitive positioning.

The Riley Tannery operations represent a valuable component of Beatrice Foods' diversified manufacturing portfolio, generating consistent profitability while maintaining strong market positioning in the regional leather industry. The facility's 54-year operational history demonstrates the stability and reliability that align with Beatrice Foods' long-term strategic objectives.

**Total Expenses:** $720,162

The financial performance of the Riley Tannery operations during 1963 reflects the continued viability of this manufacturing division within the Beatrice Foods corporate structure. The facility's consistent profitability and operational stability support its continued inclusion in our diversified manufacturing portfolio.
