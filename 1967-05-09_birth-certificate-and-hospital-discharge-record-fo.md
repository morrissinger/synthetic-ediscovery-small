#### ADMISSION HISTORY
Mrs. Kane, a 28-year-old gravida 3, para 2 female, was admitted to the obstetrical ward in active labor at 11:45 PM on May 8, 1967. Patient reported onset of regular contractions at approximately 8:00 PM at home. This pregnancy was uncomplicated throughout the prenatal period with regular monthly visits to Dr. Harrison's office. Patient's previous obstetrical history includes two living children: Kevin Jr., age 6, and Donna, age 4, both delivered at term without complications.

Patient's medical history is unremarkable. She reports no known allergies to medications. Patient is a non-smoker and denies alcohol consumption during pregnancy. Family history is significant for maternal diabetes in patient's mother. Patient resides in Woburn, Massachusetts, and reports drinking municipal water from the city water supply.

#### LABOR AND DELIVERY
Labor progressed normally with cervical dilation proceeding at expected rate. Membranes ruptured spontaneously at 6:30 AM on May 9, 1967, with clear amniotic fluid noted. Patient was transferred to the delivery room at 8:15 AM when fully dilated. 

Delivery was accomplished spontaneously at 8:30 AM without complications. A healthy female infant was delivered vertex presentation, crying immediately upon delivery. Apgar scores were 8 at one minute and 9 at five minutes. The placenta delivered intact at 8:45 AM with minimal blood loss estimated at 200 ml.

#### POSTPARTUM COURSE
Patient's postpartum course was uncomplicated. Vital signs remained stable throughout hospitalization. Patient ambulated without difficulty on postpartum day one. Breastfeeding was established successfully with adequate milk production noted. Patient received routine postpartum care including fundal massage, perineal care, and breast care instruction.

Infant examination by pediatric resident Dr. Sarah E. Collins, M.D., revealed a healthy newborn female with normal physical findings. Birth weight 6 lbs, 14 oz, length 19 inches, head circumference 13.5 inches. All reflexes present and appropriate. Infant feeding well and voiding normally.

#### DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS
Patient discharged home in good condition on May 12, 1967. Instructions provided for:

- **Activity:** Gradual return to normal activities over 6 weeks
- **Diet:** Regular diet with increased protein and fluids for breastfeeding
- **Medications:** Prenatal vitamins to continue for 6 weeks
- **Follow-up:** Return to office in 6 weeks for routine postpartum examination
- **Infant care:** Routine newborn care instructions provided
