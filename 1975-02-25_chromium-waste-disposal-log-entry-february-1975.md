## DISPOSAL METHODOLOGY

### Primary Disposal Site
**Location:** Company-owned land parcel, rear of main facility  
**Coordinates:** Approximately 200 yards northeast of main tannery building  
**Disposal Method:** Direct discharge to unlined settling ponds  
**Pond Capacity:** 15,000 gallons total across three connected basins  

The chromium-bearing waste generated from this month's hide processing operations has been transferred to our standard disposal ponds following established company procedures. The settling pond system, constructed in 1962, continues to provide adequate containment for our manufacturing byproducts while allowing natural evaporation and soil filtration processes to reduce waste volume over time.

### Secondary Containment Measures
**Overflow Protection:** Earthen berms surrounding pond perimeter  
**Monitoring Wells:** None currently installed  
**Groundwater Depth:** Estimated 8-12 feet below surface  
**Soil Composition:** Sandy loam with clay sublayer at 15-foot depth

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE ASSESSMENT

### Federal Requirements
Currently no specific federal regulations govern chromium waste disposal from tannery operations. The Environmental Protection Agency has indicated potential future rulemaking regarding heavy metal discharge standards, but no implementation timeline has been established. Company legal counsel has advised continued monitoring of federal developments while maintaining current disposal practices.

### State of Massachusetts Requirements
**Chapter 21E:** Not yet enacted (anticipated 1983)  
**Water Quality Standards:** Under review by state environmental authorities  
**Industrial Discharge Permits:** Not required for on-site disposal  
**Notification Requirements:** None specified for current waste volumes  

The Massachusetts Department of Environmental Quality Engineering has not issued specific guidance regarding chromium disposal standards for leather manufacturing facilities. Our current practices align with industry standards throughout New England and comply with all applicable state regulations as currently written.

## OPERATIONAL NOTES

### Equipment Performance
The centrifuge separation system operated at 87% efficiency during this disposal cycle, representing improved performance compared to January operations (82% efficiency). The primary settling tank required cleaning on February 20th due to accumulated chromium sludge buildup, resulting in temporary storage of approximately 400 gallons in auxiliary containers.

### Personnel Safety Measures
All disposal operations conducted with appropriate personal protective equipment including rubber gloves, safety goggles, and protective clothing. No personnel exposure incidents occurred during this disposal cycle. Monthly safety training conducted February 12th with all environmental crew members present.

### Maintenance Requirements
The pond liner system shows continued deterioration along the eastern perimeter, with visible cracking in the clay base layer. Engineering assessment scheduled for March 15th to evaluate repair options and cost estimates. Current containment remains adequate for normal operations.
